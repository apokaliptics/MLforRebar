2005 studio album by Charles Lloyd

***Jumping the Creek*** is an album by [jazz](/wiki/Jazz "Jazz") saxophonist [Charles Lloyd](/wiki/Charles_Lloyd_(jazz_musician) "Charles Lloyd (jazz musician)") recorded in January 2004 and released on [ECM](/wiki/ECM_Records "ECM Records") April the following year. The quartet features rhythm section [Geri Allen](/wiki/Geri_Allen "Geri Allen"), [Robert Hurst](/wiki/Robert_Hurst_(musician) "Robert Hurst (musician)") and [Eric Harland](/wiki/Eric_Harland "Eric Harland").[[1]](#cite_note-1)

## Reception

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jumping_the_Creek&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Reception")]

The [AllMusic](/wiki/AllMusic "AllMusic") review by Thom Jurek awarded the album 4 stars and states "this, like Lloyd's other recordings on ECM is about emotion, feeling, and a sense of peace and serenity. Lloyd uses the rough places in his improvisations, to be sure, but it is only to make the rough places plain, limpid, utterly integrated in a serene whole. On *Jumping the Creek* he succeeds seamlessly and ups his own artistic ante."[[2]](#cite_note-Allmusic-2)

The [All About Jazz](/wiki/All_About_Jazz "All About Jazz") review by John Kelman states "*Jumping the Creek* represents a clear highlight in a career filled with memorable milestones."[[3]](#cite_note-3)
