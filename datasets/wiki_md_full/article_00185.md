Place in Eastern Uganda, Uganda

**Mbale** is a city in the [Eastern Region](/wiki/Eastern_Region,_Uganda "Eastern Region, Uganda") of Uganda. It is the main municipal, administrative commercial center of [Mbale District](/wiki/Mbale_District "Mbale District") and the surrounding sub-region.[[3]](#cite_note-3R-3)

## Location

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Location")]

Mbale is approximately 225 kilometres (140 mi) northeast of [Kampala](/wiki/Kampala "Kampala"), Uganda's capital city, on an all-weather [tarmac](/wiki/Asphalt_concrete "Asphalt concrete") highway.[[4]](#cite_note-4) The city lies at an average elevation of 1,156 metres (3,793 ft) above sea level.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

The coordinates of the city are 1°04'50.0"N, 34°10'30.0"E (Latitude:1.080556; Longitude:34.175000).[[6]](#cite_note-6) The city is on the railway from [Tororo](/wiki/Tororo "Tororo") to [Pakwach](/wiki/Pakwach "Pakwach"). [Mount Elgon](/wiki/Mount_Elgon "Mount Elgon"), one of the highest peaks in [East Africa](/wiki/East_Africa "East Africa"), is approximately 48 kilometres (30 mi), north-east of Mbale, by road.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

## Population

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Population")]

According to the 2002 census, the population of Mbale was about 71,130. In 2010, the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) estimated the population at 81,900. In 2011, UBOS estimated the mid-year population at 91,800.[[8]](#cite_note-PopR-8) The 2014 population census put the population at 96,189.[[1]](#cite_note-Elgon-1)

## Twinning

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Twinning")]

Mbale was formally linked with the town of [Pontypridd](/wiki/Pontypridd "Pontypridd"), [Wales](/wiki/Wales "Wales") through local and regional twinning ceremonies in 2005. The link was intended to associate professionals and organizations in Pontypridd with their counterparts in Africa, under the auspices of the charity known as the Partnerships Overseas Networking Trust.[[9]](#cite_note-9)

## Points of interest

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Points of interest")]

The following points of interest lie within the city limits or close to its borders:

### Administrative

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Administrative")]

* The headquarters of [Mbale District Administration](/wiki/Mbale_District "Mbale District")
* The offices of Mbale City Council

### Public facilities

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Public facilities")]

* Mbale central market
* Mbale Municipal Stadium

### Military

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Military")]

* The headquarters of the 3rd Division of the [Uganda People's Defense Force](/wiki/Uganda_People%27s_Defense_Force "Uganda People's Defense Force")

### Health facilities

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Health facilities")]

* [Mbale Regional Referral Hospital](/wiki/Mbale_Hospital "Mbale Hospital") - a 400-bed public hospital administered by the [Uganda Ministry of Health](/wiki/Uganda_Ministry_of_Health "Uganda Ministry of Health")
* [CURE Children's Hospital of Uganda](/wiki/CURE_Children%27s_Hospital_of_Uganda "CURE Children's Hospital of Uganda")

### Educational institutions

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Educational institutions")]

* Uganda Christian University College - Headquartered at Mukono UCU, formerly Bishop Tucker Theological College
* [Busitema University Faculty of Health Sciences](/wiki/Busitema_University_Faculty_of_Health_Sciences "Busitema University Faculty of Health Sciences") - the medical school of [Busitema University](/wiki/Busitema_University "Busitema University"), a public institution of higher education
* The main campus of the [Islamic University in Uganda](/wiki/Islamic_University_in_Uganda "Islamic University in Uganda")
* The Mbale Campus of [Uganda Martyrs University](/wiki/Uganda_Martyrs_University "Uganda Martyrs University") - a private university, whose headquarters are located in [Nkozi](/wiki/Nkozi "Nkozi"), [Mpigi District](/wiki/Mpigi_District "Mpigi District")
* [LivingStone International University](/wiki/LivingStone_International_University "LivingStone International University") - a private university affiliated with the fellowship of [Christian Churches](/wiki/Christian_Churches "Christian Churches") and [Churches of Christ](/wiki/Churches_of_Christ "Churches of Christ")
* Mbale School of Clinical Officers

## City status

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: City status")]

In 2019 the [Cabinet of Uganda](/wiki/Cabinet_of_Uganda "Cabinet of Uganda"), resolved to award Mbale, city status effective July 2021.[[10]](#cite_note-10) In November of the same year, Cabinet revised the date of city status to 1 July 2020.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

## Notable people

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mbale&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Notable people")]

* [Semei Kakungulu](/wiki/Semei_Kakungulu "Semei Kakungulu"): a General from Kooki, who established Mbale Town in 1903
* [John Wasikye](/wiki/John_Wasikye "John Wasikye"): Anglican Bishop, was Murdered After Liberation of Kampala, April 11, 1979
* [Canon Andrew Nyote](/wiki/Canon_Andrew_Nyote "Canon Andrew Nyote"): Political and Community Leader.
* [James Wapakhabulo](/wiki/James_Wapakhabulo "James Wapakhabulo"): Politician
* [Lydia Wanyoto](/wiki/Lydia_Wanyoto "Lydia Wanyoto"): Lawyer, politician and diplomat.
* [Nathan Nandala Mafabi](/wiki/Nandala_Mafabi "Nandala Mafabi"): Ugandan accountant, lawyer, and politician. He represents Budadiri County West in [Sironko District](/wiki/Sironko_District "Sironko District") in the [Parliament of Uganda](/wiki/Parliament_of_Uganda "Parliament of Uganda"). From May 2011 until January 2014, he was the [leader of the opposition](/wiki/Leader_of_Opposition_(Uganda) "Leader of Opposition (Uganda)").
* [Walter de Sousa](/wiki/Walter_de_Sousa "Walter de Sousa") Field Hockey player for [Indian hockey team](/wiki/India_men%27s_national_field_hockey_team "India men's national field hockey team"). He was born in Mbale, Uganda and spent his early childhood here where his father was working under the colonial administration.
* [Dani Wadada Nabudere](/wiki/Dani_Wadada_Nabudere "Dani Wadada Nabudere") (15 December 1932 – 9 November 2011) was a Ugandan academic, Pan-Africanist, lawyer, politician, author, political scientist, and development specialist.
* [Gershom Sizomu](/wiki/Gershom_Sizomu "Gershom Sizomu"): First chief rabbi of Uganda, from the [Abayudaya](/wiki/Abayudaya "Abayudaya") community. Member of the [Ugandan Parliament](/wiki/Ugandan_Parliament "Ugandan Parliament") for the Bungokho North constituency.
* [Lanie Banks](/wiki/Lanie_Banks "Lanie Banks"): Canadian-Ugandan rapper, songwriter and community activist.
* [Werike Kafabusa](/wiki/Werikhe_Kafabusa "Werikhe Kafabusa"): Ugandan politician.
