## Background and early life

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tekno_(musician)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Background and early life")]

Kelechi is from Ivo Local Government Area, [Ebonyi State](/wiki/Ebonyi_State "Ebonyi State"), southeastern [Nigeria](/wiki/Nigeria "Nigeria").[[2]](#cite_note-onlinedailys.com-2) He was born in [Bauchi State](/wiki/Bauchi_State "Bauchi State") into a family of six — five boys and one girl.[[3]](#cite_note-3)[[4]](#cite_note-4) He was raised in several parts of the country including [Nasarawa State](/wiki/Nasarawa_State "Nasarawa State"), [Kaduna](/wiki/Kaduna_(city) "Kaduna (city)") (the capital city of [Kaduna State](/wiki/Kaduna_State "Kaduna State")) and [Abuja](/wiki/Abuja "Abuja") (the federal capital territory of Nigeria) due to the fact that his father was a member of the [Nigerian Army](/wiki/Nigerian_Army "Nigerian Army").[[5]](#cite_note-pulse-5) At the age of 8, Tekno Miles was enrolled in a [music school](/wiki/Music_school "Music school") where he learned and mastered the rudiments of playing the piano and guitar.[[5]](#cite_note-pulse-5) He is the elder brother to another Nigerian musical act, *Spotless*.[[6]](#cite_note-6)

## Musical career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tekno_(musician)&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Musical career")]

Tekno Miles was first signed under K-Money Entertainment. His breakout single titled "Holiday", was released under the imprint. With featured vocals from [Davido](/wiki/Davido "Davido"), "Holiday" was positively accepted and gained massive airplay.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

In 2012, Tekno Miles was spotted by renowned Nigerian comedian [Julius Agwu](/wiki/Julius_Agwu "Julius Agwu") at an event in Abuja, after Tekno Miles received a standing ovation following the performance of a song titled "Onye Ne Kwu", his remix of [Ice Prince](/wiki/Ice_Prince "Ice Prince")'s "Oleku". It was at the same event he met [Iyanya](/wiki/Iyanya "Iyanya") and Ubi Franklin, the manager of Made Men Music Group and they became friends. Ubi and [Iyanya](/wiki/Iyanya "Iyanya") eventually encouraged Tekno Miles to move to [Lagos](/wiki/Lagos "Lagos") to further his music career.

On 5 October 2013, he signed a [recording contract](/wiki/Recording_contract "Recording contract") with Made Men Music Group *(MMMG)*,[[8]](#cite_note-8) under which he released singles like "Dance" and "Anything". These singles earned him a nomination in the "Best New act of the Year" category at the [2014 Nigeria Entertainment Awards](/wiki/2014_Nigeria_Entertainment_Awards "2014 Nigeria Entertainment Awards").[[9]](#cite_note-9) On 18 June 2015, Tekno Miles released his hit single titled "Duro", which was produced by [DJ Coublon](/wiki/DJ_Coublon "DJ Coublon"). "Duro" was positively received both in Africa and in the US.[[10]](#cite_note-10) A remix which featured [Phyno](/wiki/Phyno "Phyno") and [Flavour N'abania](/wiki/Flavour_N%27abania "Flavour N'abania") was released on 16 November 2015.[[11]](#cite_note-11)[[12]](#cite_note-12) It topped several charts in Nigeria and on the international scene, it peaked at number 5 on [Capital XTRA](/wiki/Capital_XTRA "Capital XTRA")'s Afrobeats Chart: Top 10 for September 2015.[[13]](#cite_note-13) On 20 November 2015, Tekno Miles released a single titled "Wash". The song was produced by [DJ Coublon](/wiki/DJ_Coublon "DJ Coublon") with the video directed by AJE Filmworks.[[14]](#cite_note-14) On 27 December 2016, Tekno was named one of the top ten hip-hop and R&B talents to watch in the United States in 2017 by [Billboard](/wiki/Billboard_(magazine) "Billboard (magazine)").[[15]](#cite_note-15) tekno also known as the Pana master has changed his name to [Big Tek](https://xclusivetones.com/tekno-changes-his-stage-name-to-big-tek/)

In 2016, [Elton John](/wiki/Elton_John "Elton John") played Tekno song titled 'Diana' on one of his episode of Rocket Hour radio show on [Apple Music](/wiki/Apple_Music "Apple Music").[[16]](#cite_note-16)

However, On 13 October 2017, Tekno was featured by DJ Cuppy on a song titled *Green Light*, which was officially DJ Cuppy's first single.[[17]](#cite_note-17)

On 12 December 2017,[[18]](#cite_note-18) Canadian rapper [Drake](/wiki/Drake_(musician) "Drake (musician)") posted images of himself with Tekno to his Instagram page, captioning them, "Tekkk time."[[19]](#cite_note-19)[[20]](#cite_note-20)

On 10 August 2018,[[21]](#cite_note-21) American singer Ciara, has teamed up with Tekno, for a collaboration titled "Freak Me."[[22]](#cite_note-22) Tekno announced the song's release and revealed the cover art on his Instagram account @teknoofficial on Friday.[[23]](#cite_note-23)

On 15 August 2019,[[24]](#cite_note-24) Tekno was announced as one of the producers of ‘Won't Be Late’ by Swae Lee featuring Drake

In June 2018, Tekno signed a distribution deal with Universal Music Group Nigeria (UMGN), a division of Universal Music Group in charge of West Africa, as well as the UK-based Island Records.[[25]](#cite_note-25) In July 2019, Tekno was featured on [Beyoncé](/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9 "Beyoncé")'s *[The Lion King: The Gift](/wiki/The_Lion_King:_The_Gift "The Lion King: The Gift")* soundtrack on the song "Don't Jealous Me".[[26]](#cite_note-26)

On 6 December 2020, Tekno released the tracklist for his long-awaited debut album, *Old Romance*.[[27]](#cite_note-27) In 2022, he featured in Kizz Daniel's '**[Buga](/wiki/Buga_(song) "Buga (song)")'**, a track that became the No. 1 shazamed tune within the same year.[[28]](#cite_note-28) Also, within the same year, [Davido](/wiki/Davido "Davido") revealed how Tekno had assisted in reviving his career at a very crucial moment when he needed fresh inspiration.[[29]](#cite_note-29)

Afro-pop singer Tekno has released the music video for his latest single "Puttin".[[30]](#cite_note-30)
On the back of his acclaimed 2023 album, The More The Better, award -winning Nigerian musician and producer, Tekno, has kicked off a new musical era with his latest song, dubbed, Wayo. This new single is the first offering of his new partnership with Mr Eazi's emPawa Africa, which will see Tekno becoming an investor and partner in the label.[[31]](#cite_note-31)

## Personal life

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Tekno_(musician)&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Personal life")]

In May 2018, Tekno's girlfriend gave birth to a girl child.[[32]](#cite_note-32) In 2020, Tekno announced that he has relocated to the United States.[[33]](#cite_note-33) This was a reaction to the news of the proposal presented at the Nigerian National Assembly for the change of the country's name to UAR, which was an acronym for United African Republic or United Alkebulan Republic on 2 June.

In 2017, Tekno made it known to the public that he has acid reflux. The condition had gotten complicated and had ultimately affected his vocal cords. He had to undergo surgery to tackle the issue.[[34]](#cite_note-34)
