American psychologist, author, and professor

**Jennifer Aaker** (born January 15, 1967) is an American [behavioural scientist](/wiki/Behavioural_scientist "Behavioural scientist") and General Atlantic Professor and Coulter Family Fellow at the [Stanford Graduate School of Business](/wiki/Stanford_Graduate_School_of_Business "Stanford Graduate School of Business"). She is known for her research on time, money, and happiness.[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) Aaker also focuses on the transmission of ideas through social networks, the power of story in decision making, and how to build global brands across cultures.[[3]](#cite_note-fast1-3) She is the recipient of the Distinguished Scientific Achievement Award from the [Society for Consumer Psychology](/wiki/Society_for_Consumer_Psychology "Society for Consumer Psychology") and the Stanford Distinguished Teaching Award.[[4]](#cite_note-4)

## Early life and education

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jennifer_Aaker&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")]

Aaker was born in [Palo Alto, California](/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California "Palo Alto, California") to Kay Aaker[[5]](#cite_note-5) and [David Aaker](/wiki/David_Aaker "David Aaker"), a professor and brand consultant.[[6]](#cite_note-6) Aaker attended the [University of California, Berkeley](/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley "University of California, Berkeley"), where she studied under social psychologist [Philip E. Tetlock](/wiki/Philip_E._Tetlock "Philip E. Tetlock") and Nobel Prize winner [Daniel Kahneman](/wiki/Daniel_Kahneman "Daniel Kahneman"), receiving a Bachelor of Arts in [Psychology](/wiki/Psychology "Psychology") in 1989. In 1990, Aaker began postgraduate work at Stanford Graduate School of Business, earning a PhD in marketing with a minor in psychology in 1995. Her dissertation on brand personality led to the publication of academic papers in *[Journal of Marketing Research](/wiki/Journal_of_Marketing_Research "Journal of Marketing Research")* and *[Journal of Consumer Research](/wiki/Journal_of_Consumer_Research "Journal of Consumer Research")*.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

## Career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jennifer_Aaker&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Career")]

Aaker began her academic career in 1995 as an assistant professor at the [UCLA Anderson School of Management](/wiki/UCLA_Anderson_School_of_Management "UCLA Anderson School of Management"). In 1999, she returned to the Stanford Graduate School of Business (GSB) as an [assistant professor](/wiki/Assistant_professor "Assistant professor"), was promoted to [associate professor](/wiki/Associate_professor "Associate professor") in 2001, and earned a full professorship in 2004. In 2005, Aaker was named General Atlantic Professor and Coulter Family Fellow, Stanford GSB.[[8]](#cite_note-8) Her work has been published in scholarly journals in psychology and marketing and has been highlighted in *[The Economist](/wiki/The_Economist "The Economist")*, *[The New York Times](/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")*, *[The Wall Street Journal](/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal "The Wall Street Journal")*, *[The Washington Post](/wiki/The_Washington_Post "The Washington Post")*, *[BusinessWeek](/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek "Bloomberg Businessweek")*, *[Forbes](/wiki/Forbes "Forbes"),* [NPR](/wiki/NPR "NPR"), *[CBS MoneyWatch](/wiki/CBS_MoneyWatch "CBS MoneyWatch")*, *[Inc.](/wiki/Inc._(magazine) "Inc. (magazine)")*, and *[Science](/wiki/Science_(journal) "Science (journal)").* She serves as an advisory board member for several private and public companies.[*[verification needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability "Wikipedia:Verifiability")*]

In 2010, Aaker and her husband, startup advisor Andy Smith, wrote *The Dragonfly Effect: Quick, Effective and Powerful Ways to Use Social Media to Drive Social Change.*[[9]](#cite_note-9)

In a real world demonstration of the Dragonfly Effect, Aaker and her students founded 100K Cheeks, an organization dedicated to registering 100,000 South Asian donors in the [National Bone Marrow Registry](/wiki/National_Bone_Marrow_Registry "National Bone Marrow Registry").[[3]](#cite_note-fast1-3) In addition to using social networks, Aaker ran the first ever cheek swab in India. As a result of these efforts, 100K Cheeks exceeded their goal by registering more than 115,000 potential donors.[[10]](#cite_note-10)[[11]](#cite_note-11)[[12]](#cite_note-dragonfly-12)

In 2021, Aaker published *Humor, Seriously: Why Humor Is A Secret Weapon in Business and Life* with co-author Naomi Bagdonas.[[13]](#cite_note-13)
