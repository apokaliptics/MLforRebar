Australian rugby league referee

**Steven Clark** is a former [Australian](/wiki/Australia "Australia") professional [referee](/wiki/Referee_(rugby_league) "Referee (rugby league)") in the [National Rugby League](/wiki/National_Rugby_League "National Rugby League") competition.

## Background

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Steve_Clark_(referee)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Background")]

Clark was born in [New South Wales](/wiki/New_South_Wales "New South Wales"), [Australia](/wiki/Australia "Australia").

## NRL career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Steve_Clark_(referee)&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: NRL career")]

Clark made his first grade debut in the match between [Parramatta Eels](/wiki/Parramatta_Eels "Parramatta Eels") and the [Newcastle Knights](/wiki/Newcastle_Knights "Newcastle Knights"), at [Parramatta Stadium](/wiki/Parramatta_Stadium "Parramatta Stadium") on 31 May 1992. In 2007 Clark reached the 300 first grade game barrier becoming only the third referee to do so. At the time of his retirement only [Bill Harrigan](/wiki/Bill_Harrigan "Bill Harrigan") with 393 games and [Col Pearce](/wiki/Col_Pearce "Col Pearce") with 343 games had refereed more matches than him.[[1]](#cite_note-Tele-1)

## Representative honours

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Steve_Clark_(referee)&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Representative honours")]

Clark made his representative refereeing debut in 1996, when he officiated the [Test match](/wiki/Rugby_League_International_Federation#Competitions "Rugby League International Federation") between [Great Britain](/wiki/Great_Britain_national_rugby_league_team "Great Britain national rugby league team") and [New Zealand](/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team "New Zealand national rugby league team").

In 2005 he controlled in his one and only [City vs Country Origin](/wiki/City_vs_Country_Origin "City vs Country Origin") match at [Northpower Stadium](/wiki/Bluetongue_Central_Coast_Stadium "Bluetongue Central Coast Stadium") in [Gosford](/wiki/Gosford,_New_South_Wales "Gosford, New South Wales").

Clark made his first [State of Origin](/wiki/Rugby_League_State_of_Origin "Rugby League State of Origin") appearance in [1999](/wiki/1999_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series "1999 Rugby League State of Origin series"), refereeing two matches including the first ever drawn match in [Game III](/wiki/2005_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series#Game_Three "2005 Rugby League State of Origin series"). He didn't referee again until the [2005 series](/wiki/2005_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series "2005 Rugby League State of Origin series") where he was appointed to [Game II](/wiki/2005_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series#Game_Two "2005 Rugby League State of Origin series"). In the [following series](/wiki/2006_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series "2006 Rugby League State of Origin series") he controlled Games [II](/wiki/2006_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series#Game_Two "2006 Rugby League State of Origin series") and [III](/wiki/2006_Rugby_League_State_of_Origin_series#Game_Three "2006 Rugby League State of Origin series").[[1]](#cite_note-Tele-1)
