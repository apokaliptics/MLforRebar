Dominican professional wrestler and politician (1942–2021)

**Jack Veneno** ("Jack the Venom"; born **Rafael Antonio Sánchez**; May 2, 1942 – April 6, 2021) was a [Dominican](/wiki/List_of_people_from_the_Dominican_Republic "List of people from the Dominican Republic") [professional wrestler](/wiki/Professional_wrestling "Professional wrestling") and [politician](/wiki/Politician "Politician").[[2]](#cite_note-2)[[3]](#cite_note-3)

## Professional wrestling career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jack_Veneno&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Professional wrestling career")]

He became interested in wrestling as a teen, watching the [Mexican](/wiki/Mexican_people "Mexican people") [luchador](/wiki/Luchador "Luchador") [El Santo](/wiki/El_Santo "El Santo").[[4]](#cite_note-en1-4) He debuted in 1969. Also known as "El hijo de Doña Tatica" (Mrs. Tatica's Son), he worked for many years on [Color Visión](/wiki/Color_Visi%C3%B3n "Color Visión") channel 9 during the 1970s, 1980s and 1990s, on a wrestling show called *Lucha Libre Internacional*.[[5]](#cite_note-desig-5) He was the main star of the show as well as the owner of the production company *Dominicana de Espectaculos*.

On September 7, 1982, Veneno challenged [Ric Flair](/wiki/Ric_Flair "Ric Flair") for the [NWA World Heavyweight Championship](/wiki/NWA_World_Heavyweight_Championship "NWA World Heavyweight Championship") in the [Palacio de los Deportes Virgilio Travieso Soto](/wiki/Palacio_de_los_Deportes_Virgilio_Travieso_Soto "Palacio de los Deportes Virgilio Travieso Soto") in [Santo Domingo](/wiki/Santo_Domingo "Santo Domingo") in front of an audience of circa 30,000 people.[[6]](#cite_note-Kreikenbohm1982-6) The match ended when Veneno applied his signature [sleeper hold](/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Sleeper_hold "Professional wrestling holds") to Flair, with the bell ringing to signify the expiration of the match's time limit as Flair's arm dropped for a third time. The fans then celebrated Veneno winning the NWA World Heavyweight Championship.[[7]](#cite_note-Flair-7) The title change was not recognized by the National Wrestling Alliance. Flair reportedly instructed Veneno to defeat him due to fears of the audience in the oversold arena turning violent if the popular Veneno lost.[[8]](#cite_note-Meltzer-8)[[9]](#cite_note-Jacobs2019-9)[[10]](#cite_note-Letawsky-10) In the Dominican Republic, Veneno was reported to have surrendered the title back to Flair due to not being willing to leave the country to defend it.[[9]](#cite_note-Jacobs2019-9)

Veneno retired himself in 2000.[[4]](#cite_note-en1-4)

In 2007 a trailer for a documentary featuring the lost footage of his match with Flair aired throughout the Dominican Republic, but was not released at that time. In April 2015 the documentary was finally released on the internet for free, and shows the only footage known to exist of the famous match, the film "Jack; La Historia de Jack Veneno" features Jack and others discussing his career and life. The film was produced by Benjamin Irish, a protégé of Veneno, and is widely attributed to Veneno's resurgence of celebrity in the country after 2007.

## Political career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jack_Veneno&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Political career")]

Although retired from wrestling, Veneno ran for Mayor of [Santo Domingo Norte](/wiki/Santo_Domingo_Norte "Santo Domingo Norte"), one of the [Santo Domingo Province](/wiki/Santo_Domingo_Province "Santo Domingo Province") municipalities, on the [2006 Congressional and Municipal Elections](/wiki/2006_Dominican_Republic_parliamentary_election "2006 Dominican Republic parliamentary election") with the [Institutional Social Democratic Bloc](/wiki/Institutional_Social_Democratic_Bloc "Institutional Social Democratic Bloc") party.[[4]](#cite_note-en1-4) In 2007, he was appointed by the Dominican Republic president [Leonel Fernández](/wiki/Leonel_Fern%C3%A1ndez "Leonel Fernández") as Vice-Minister of Sports.[[5]](#cite_note-desig-5)[[11]](#cite_note-11)

## Legacy and death

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Jack_Veneno&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Legacy and death")]

A movie trilogy about Veneno's life is planned; [the first part](/wiki/Veneno_(film) "Veneno (film)") was released in 2018.[[12]](#cite_note-12)

On April 13, 2019, a statue was unveiled in Veneno's honor in the [Eugenio María de Hostos](/wiki/Eugenio_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Hostos "Eugenio María de Hostos") Park in Santo Domingo.[[13]](#cite_note-13)

On April 6, 2021, Veneno died from [pancreatic cancer](/wiki/Pancreatic_cancer "Pancreatic cancer"), which had metastasized to his lungs. He was 78 years old.[[14]](#cite_note-14)
