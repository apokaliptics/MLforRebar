Canadian biochemist

**Benoît Roux** is an [Amgen](/wiki/Amgen "Amgen") Professor of [Biochemistry](/wiki/Biochemistry "Biochemistry") and [Molecular Biophysics](/wiki/Molecular_Biophysics "Molecular Biophysics") at the [University of Chicago](/wiki/University_of_Chicago "University of Chicago"). He has previously taught at [University of Montreal](/wiki/University_of_Montreal "University of Montreal") and [Weill Medical College of Cornell University](/wiki/Weill_Medical_College_of_Cornell_University "Weill Medical College of Cornell University"). Benoît Roux was a recipient of the 1998 [Rutherford Memorial Medal](/wiki/Rutherford_Memorial_Medal "Rutherford Memorial Medal") in [Chemistry](/wiki/Chemistry "Chemistry"), awarded by the [Royal Society of Canada](/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Canada "Royal Society of Canada").[[2]](#cite_note-2)

## Life and career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Beno%C3%AEt_Roux&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Life and career")]

Roux obtained B.Sc. and M.Sc. in [physics](/wiki/Physics "Physics") from the [University of Montreal](/wiki/University_of_Montreal "University of Montreal") in 1981 and 1984 respectively. He completed his Ph.D. at [Harvard University](/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University") under the supervision of [Martin Karplus](/wiki/Martin_Karplus "Martin Karplus"), graduating in 1990. He served at the [French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission](/wiki/French_Alternative_Energies_and_Atomic_Energy_Commission "French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission") from 1991 to 1992 and was a Foreign Research Fellow at the Centre D’Etudes. He was a faculty member in the department of physics at the Université de Montréal until 1999, when he relocated to [Weill Cornell Medicine](/wiki/Weill_Cornell_Medicine "Weill Cornell Medicine"). He relocated to the department of biochemistry and molecular biology at the [University of Chicago](/wiki/University_of_Chicago "University of Chicago") in 2005. He also serves as a research scientist at the [Center for Nanoscale Materials](/wiki/Center_for_Nanoscale_Materials "Center for Nanoscale Materials"), a department of the [Argonne National Laboratory](/wiki/Argonne_National_Laboratory "Argonne National Laboratory").[[3]](#cite_note-3) He is an accomplished classical pianist, specializing in the work of [Frédéric Chopin](/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin "Frédéric Chopin").

## Research

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Beno%C3%AEt_Roux&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Research")]

His laboratory at the [University of Chicago](/wiki/University_of_Chicago "University of Chicago") mostly uses theoretical techniques, such as classical [molecular dynamics](/wiki/Molecular_dynamics "Molecular dynamics"), to understand the functioning of [biological systems](/wiki/Biological_system "Biological system") at the molecular level. His research has investigated structure, dynamics, and the function of biological [macromolecular systems](/wiki/Macromolecule "Macromolecule") such as [ion channels](/wiki/Ion_channels "Ion channels"), [receptors](/wiki/Receptor_(biochemistry) "Receptor (biochemistry)"), and [protein kinases](/wiki/Protein_kinases "Protein kinases").[[4]](#cite_note-4)

He is a pioneer in the study of [membrane proteins](/wiki/Membrane_protein "Membrane protein") using [molecular dynamics](/wiki/Molecular_dynamics "Molecular dynamics") with explicit [phospholipid molecules](/wiki/Phospholipid "Phospholipid") and solvent.[[5]](#cite_note-5)[[6]](#cite_note-6)
His laboratory has also developed novel computational methods to improve efficiency and applicability of theoretical investigations to [molecular recognition](/wiki/Molecular_recognition "Molecular recognition") phenomena. His work has bridged theory and experiment in [biophysics](/wiki/Biophysics "Biophysics") by employing ever-increasing [computational power](/wiki/Moore%27s_law "Moore's law") to further the understanding of the molecular [basis of life](/wiki/History_of_biology "History of biology").

## Works

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Beno%C3%AEt_Roux&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Works")]

In 1996, he co-authored *Biological Membranes: A Molecular Perspective from Computation and Experiment* with Kenneth M. Merz.[[7]](#cite_note-7) In 2001, he co-edited a book with Oren M. Becker, Alexander D. MacKerell Jr., Benoit Roux, Masakatsu Watanabe, *Computational Biochemistry and Biophysics*. [[8]](#cite_note-8) In 2021, he authored a text on biophysical theory and simulations, *Computational Modeling and Simulations of Biomolecular Systems*.[[9]](#cite_note-9)
