The ***Economic Information Daily*** ([simplified Chinese](/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 经济参考报; [traditional Chinese](/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "Traditional Chinese characters"): 經濟參考報; [pinyin](/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Jīngjì Cānkǎo Bào*[[3]](#cite_note-Ng2018-3)), also translated into English as ***Economic Reference Paper***[[4]](#cite_note-Ye2014-4) or ***Economic Reference Daily***,[[5]](#cite_note-FanHeberer2015-5) is a Chinese state-run [newspaper](/wiki/Newspaper "Newspaper")[[6]](#cite_note-6) specializing in [economics](/wiki/Economics "Economics"),[[7]](#cite_note-7) based in [Beijing](/wiki/Beijing "Beijing").

*Economic Information Daily* is sponsored and supervised by [Xinhua News Agency](/wiki/Xinhua_News_Agency "Xinhua News Agency"),[[8]](#cite_note-OtmazginBen-Ari2013-8) and is directly contacted and directed by the [Central Policy Research Office of China](/wiki/Central_Policy_Research_Office "Central Policy Research Office").[[9]](#cite_note-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10)

Inaugurated in [Beijing](/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") on 1 July 1981,[[11]](#cite_note-11) *Economic Information Daily* is the first national economic professional newspaper born during China's [Reform and opening up](/wiki/Reform_and_opening_up "Reform and opening up").[[12]](#cite_note-12) On 7 December 1990, [Deng Xiaoping](/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping") wrote the name of the *Economic Information Daily* in his own [handwriting](/wiki/Handwriting "Handwriting").[[13]](#cite_note-13)

On July 17, 2013, [Wang Wenzhi](/w/index.php?title=Wang_Wenzhi&action=edit&redlink=1 "Wang Wenzhi (page does not exist)") (王文志), the principal reporter of the *Economic Information Daily*, reported in his [Sina Weibo](/wiki/Sina_Weibo "Sina Weibo") that the chairman of [China Resources Group](/wiki/China_Resources_Group "China Resources Group"), [Song Lin](/wiki/Song_Lin "Song Lin"), was suspected of huge corruption.[[14]](#cite_note-14) Wang accused Song Lin of intentionally overpaying for a coal-industry acquisition, resulting in the loss of billions of state property.[[15]](#cite_note-BabcockFreivogel2015-15)
