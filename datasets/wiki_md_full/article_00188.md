The **Empire State League** was a [minor league baseball](/wiki/Minor_league_baseball "Minor league baseball") league that played from 1905 to 1907. The 14 different league franchises were based exclusively in [New York (state)](/wiki/New_York_(state) "New York (state)"). The league name was succeeded by the 1913 [Empire State League](/wiki/Empire_State_League "Empire State League") based in Georgia and [Empire State League (1987)](/wiki/Empire_State_League_(1987) "Empire State League (1987)") based in [New York (state)](/wiki/New_York_(state) "New York (state)").

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Empire_State_League_(1905%E2%80%931907)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

On March 22, 1905, the Empire State League was formed at a meeting held in [Syracuse, New York](/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York "Syracuse, New York"). The teams were selected and R.C. Mayor of Rome was elected secretary and treasurer. Salaries were set at $600.00 per month for each team.[[1]](#cite_note-1)

The 1905 Empire State League began play as an eight–team independent league on May 26, 1905. Seneca Falls won the championship with a 42–26 record and several teams folded or relocated during the 1905 season.[[2]](#cite_note-auto-2)[[3]](#cite_note-auto2-3)

In 1906, the Empire State League reduced to six teams, beginning the season on May 26, 1906 and becoming a [Class D](/wiki/Class_D_(baseball) "Class D (baseball)") level minor league.[[4]](#cite_note-auto1-4)[[5]](#cite_note-5) With a record of 48–26, Seneca Falls again won the championship.[[6]](#cite_note-auto3-6)[[7]](#cite_note-encyc-7)

In its final season of play, the 1907 Empire State League continued play as a six–team Class D level league and crowned a new champion.[[8]](#cite_note-8)[[4]](#cite_note-auto1-4) The Oswego Starchmakers finished 1.0 game ahead of second place Seneca Falls to win the league championship.[[9]](#cite_note-auto4-9)[[7]](#cite_note-encyc-7)

M.T. Roche served as president of the Empire State League for all three seasons of play.[[10]](#cite_note-10)[[7]](#cite_note-encyc-7)
