Czech illustrator, graphic novelist and painter

**Helena Bochořáková-Dittrichová** (31 July 1894 – 28 March 1980) was a [Czech](/wiki/Czechs "Czechs") illustrator, [graphic novelist](/wiki/Graphic_novel "Graphic novel"), and later a painter. She is widely acknowledged as being the first female graphic novelist.[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-legacy2-2)[[3]](#cite_note-3)[[4]](#cite_note-4)

## Education and career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Helena_Bocho%C5%99%C3%A1kov%C3%A1-Dittrichov%C3%A1&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Education and career")]

Bochořáková-Dittrichová was born in a middle-class family in [Vyškov](/wiki/Vy%C5%A1kov "Vyškov"), [Moravia](/wiki/Moravia "Moravia"), [Austria-Hungary](/wiki/Austria-Hungary "Austria-Hungary") (now in the [Czech Republic](/wiki/Czech_Republic "Czech Republic")). She moved with her family to [Brno](/wiki/Brno "Brno") in 1913, where she spent the rest of her life.[[2]](#cite_note-legacy2-2)

In 1919 she began studying painting and drawing at the [Academy of Fine Arts in Prague](/wiki/Academy_of_Fine_Arts,_Prague "Academy of Fine Arts, Prague"). Upon graduating with a first prize[[5]](#cite_note-:2-5) in 1923, she received a Ministry of Education scholarship to study modern printmaking in [Paris](/wiki/Paris "Paris"). It was there that she discovered the woodcut novels of pioneering Belgian artist [Frans Masereel](/wiki/Frans_Masereel "Frans Masereel"), who greatly influenced her future work.[[6]](#cite_note-life-6)

Bochořáková-Dittrichová was a founding member of the KVU Alše Brno (visual artists' group).[[5]](#cite_note-:2-5)

Between 1924 and 1930, Bochořáková-Dittrichová had regular exhibitions at the [Salon des Indépendants](/wiki/Salon_des_Ind%C3%A9pendants "Salon des Indépendants") in Paris, as well exhibiting in Antwerp (1925), Philadelphia (1926), Zurich (1927), Buenos Aires (1928), and Vienna (1934). She was a passionate traveller and journeyed extensively throughout Europe, Russia and the United States until the outbreak of the [Second World War](/wiki/Second_World_War "Second World War").

She died in Brno at the age 85. She remains known as one of the leading printmakers and illustrators of the Czech Republic. Her art is showcased in many collections in the Czech Republic, including the Moravian Gallery, Museum Vyskovska, the Academy of Fine Arts in Prague, and the Brno City Museum.[[2]](#cite_note-legacy2-2)

## Influences

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Helena_Bocho%C5%99%C3%A1kov%C3%A1-Dittrichov%C3%A1&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Influences")]

Bochořáková-Dittrichová was influenced by the [Flemish](/wiki/Flemish_people "Flemish people") artist [Frans Masereel](/wiki/Frans_Masereel "Frans Masereel"), while studying in [Paris](/wiki/Paris "Paris")[[7]](#cite_note-:0-7) Masereel and other graphic novelists at the time were addressing issues of oppression and social injustice in their works; however, Bochořáková-Dittrichová's work was distinct, presenting the realistic and day-to-day lives of middle-class families, and also domestic issues that resembled her own life and upbringing.[[7]](#cite_note-:0-7)[[8]](#cite_note-:1-8)

Bochořáková-Dittrichová's art style is resonant with the contemporary trends in a number of European authors – [Käthe Kollwitz](/wiki/K%C3%A4the_Kollwitz "Käthe Kollwitz"), [Ernst Barlach](/wiki/Ernst_Barlach "Ernst Barlach") and [Frank Brangwyn](/wiki/Frank_Brangwyn "Frank Brangwyn").[[6]](#cite_note-life-6)

## Major works

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Helena_Bocho%C5%99%C3%A1kov%C3%A1-Dittrichov%C3%A1&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Major works")]

Her first work, *Z Mého Dětství* (*From my Childhood*), published in 1929,[[9]](#cite_note-9) is considered to be the first graphic novel (or wordless novel) published by a woman.[[10]](#cite_note-10) The work consists of a story about the day-to-day activities of a sheltered girl in a middle-class family, and is told entirely through woodcuts.[[7]](#cite_note-:0-7) The book was later published in an edition of 300 by the [British](/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom") bookshop and gallery A. Zwemmer, formerly of [Charing Cross Road](/wiki/Charing_Cross_Road "Charing Cross Road") [London](/wiki/London "London"). Her other major work, *Malířka Na Cestách* (*The Painter on the Road*), which was unpublished, presents 52 woodcuts and is likely an autobiography of the artists own journey.[[8]](#cite_note-:1-8)

## Collections

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Helena_Bocho%C5%99%C3%A1kov%C3%A1-Dittrichov%C3%A1&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Collections")]

The largest collection of Bochořáková-Dittrichová prints, drawings, and books outside of the Czech Republic is held at [Houghton Library](/wiki/Houghton_Library "Houghton Library") at Harvard University. Among the over 400 items in the collection is the second known copy of her unpublished graphic memoir, *Malířka Na Cestách* (*The Painter on the Road*).
