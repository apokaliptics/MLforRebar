The estuaries of the [River Ribble](/wiki/River_Ribble "River Ribble") and [River Alt](/wiki/River_Alt "River Alt") lie on the [Irish Sea](/wiki/Irish_Sea "Irish Sea") coasts of [Lancashire](/wiki/Lancashire "Lancashire") and [Merseyside](/wiki/Merseyside "Merseyside") in [North West England](/wiki/North_West_England "North West England"). Together they, and the area of salt marsh, mudflats, and sand dunes between them, form a [Special Protection Area](/wiki/Special_Protection_Area "Special Protection Area") and [Ramsar site](/wiki/Ramsar_site "Ramsar site") which covers the coastline between [Crosby](/wiki/Crosby,_Merseyside "Crosby, Merseyside") and [Lytham St Annes](/wiki/Lytham_St_Annes "Lytham St Annes"). These protected areas overlap with two [sites of special scientific interest](/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest "Site of Special Scientific Interest"), Ribble Estuary and [Sefton Coast](/wiki/Sefton_Coast "Sefton Coast").

## Protected area

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ribble_and_Alt_Estuaries&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Protected area")]

A large number of different species of waders and wildfowl, listed below, use the estuaries as feeding and over-wintering areas. This wide variety of bird species has led to the estuaries being officially designated as a [Special Protection Area](/wiki/Special_Protection_Area "Special Protection Area") and as a [Ramsar site](/wiki/Ramsar_site "Ramsar site").[[1]](#cite_note-Ramsar-1) The Ribble Estuary has been designated a [Site of Special Scientific Interest](/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest "Site of Special Scientific Interest") since 1966 and is now covered by Natural England's Ribble Estuary [National Nature Reserve](/wiki/National_nature_reserve_(United_Kingdom) "National nature reserve (United Kingdom)").[[2]](#cite_note-citation-2)

## The site

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ribble_and_Alt_Estuaries&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: The site")]

The [Ribble Marshes National Nature Reserve](/w/index.php?title=Ribble_Marshes_National_Nature_Reserve&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ribble Marshes National Nature Reserve (page does not exist)") is 2,302 ha (5,688 acres) in extent and it is located in the middle of the [SSSI](/wiki/SSSI "SSSI") which extends to 9,226 ha (22,798 acres). There are extensive areas of intertidal sand and silt flats, and expanses of saltmarsh. The mudflats have a large invertebrate fauna on which the waders and waterbirds feed. The saltmarshes are dominated by [saltmarsh grass](/wiki/Puccinellia_maritima "Puccinellia maritima") and [red fescue](/wiki/Festuca_rubra "Festuca rubra") with [cord-grass](/wiki/Spartina "Spartina") on the seaward edge.[[2]](#cite_note-citation-2) The [Ramsar](/wiki/Ramsar_Convention "Ramsar Convention") wetlands extend to 13,464 ha (33,270 acres) and include an area of sand dunes which has interesting vegetation and provides habitat for important populations of amphibians. The whole site is of great importance to internationally important numbers of wintering waterbirds.[[1]](#cite_note-Ramsar-1)

## Species

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ribble_and_Alt_Estuaries&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Species")]

Birds that breed at the site include [common tern](/wiki/Common_tern "Common tern") (*Sterna hirundo*), [lesser black-backed gull](/wiki/Lesser_black-backed_gull "Lesser black-backed gull") (*Larus fuscus*) and [ruff](/wiki/Ruff_(bird) "Ruff (bird)") (*Philomachus pugnax*).[[3]](#cite_note-RIS-3) Migratory species that visit in spring and autumn include [ringed plover](/wiki/Common_ringed_plover "Common ringed plover") (*Charadrius hiaticula*) and [sanderling](/wiki/Sanderling "Sanderling") (*Calidris alba*).[[3]](#cite_note-RIS-3)

Birds that over-winter here include [bar-tailed godwit](/wiki/Bar-tailed_godwit "Bar-tailed godwit") (*Limosa lapponica*), [black-tailed godwit](/wiki/Black-tailed_godwit "Black-tailed godwit") (*Limosa limosa islandica*), [Bewick's swan](/wiki/Tundra_swan "Tundra swan") (*Cygnus columbianus bewickii*), [dunlin](/wiki/Dunlin "Dunlin") (*Calidris alpina alpina*), [golden plover](/wiki/European_golden_plover "European golden plover") (*Pluvialis apricaria*), [grey plover](/wiki/Grey_plover "Grey plover") (*Pluvialis squatarola*), [knot](/wiki/Red_knot "Red knot") (*Calidris canutus*), [oystercatcher](/wiki/Eurasian_oystercatcher "Eurasian oystercatcher") (*Haematopus ostralegus*), [pink-footed goose](/wiki/Pink-footed_goose "Pink-footed goose") (*Anser brachyrhynchus*), [pintail](/wiki/Northern_pintail "Northern pintail") (*Anas acuta*), [redshank](/wiki/Common_redshank "Common redshank") (*Tringa totanus*), [sanderling](/wiki/Sanderling "Sanderling") (*Calidris alba*), [shelduck](/wiki/Common_shelduck "Common shelduck") (*Tadorna tadorna*), [teal](/wiki/Eurasian_teal "Eurasian teal") (*Anas crecca*), [whooper swan](/wiki/Whooper_swan "Whooper swan") (*Cygnus cygnus*) and [Eurasian wigeon](/wiki/Eurasian_wigeon "Eurasian wigeon") (*Anas penelope*).[[3]](#cite_note-RIS-3)
