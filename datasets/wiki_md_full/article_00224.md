School in Kwekwe, Midlands Province, Zimbabwe

**Goldridge College** (or **Goldridge**) is a [co-educational](/wiki/Co-educational "Co-educational"), [independent](/wiki/Independent_school "Independent school"), [boarding](/wiki/Boarding_school "Boarding school") and [day](/wiki/Day_school "Day school") senior school in [Kwekwe](/wiki/Kwekwe "Kwekwe"), [Zimbabwe](/wiki/Zimbabwe "Zimbabwe"). Opened in 2001, the school is located in the low-density suburb of Newtown and is in close proximity to [Kwekwe Sports Club](/wiki/Kwekwe_Sports_Club "Kwekwe Sports Club").[[2]](#cite_note-Goldridge_Welcome-2) The school is run by the Goldridge Schools Board of Governors, which similarly runs the nearby feeder school, Goldridge Primary.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

Goldridge College is a member of the [Association of Trust Schools](/wiki/Association_of_Trust_Schools "Association of Trust Schools") (ATS).The headmaster is a member of the Conference of Heads of Independent Schools in Zimbabwe (CHISZ).[[4]](#cite_note-ATS_Directory-4)

## Academics

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Goldridge_College&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Academics")]

Goldridge College is a registered [Cambridge International Examinations](/wiki/Cambridge_International_Examinations "Cambridge International Examinations") (CIE) centre. Students write the CIE [Checkpoint](http://www.cie.org.uk/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-secondary-1/cambridge-checkpoint/), [IGCSE](http://www.cie.org.uk/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-secondary-2/cambridge-igcse/), AS Level and [A Level](/wiki/A-level "A-level") examinations.

## Sport

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Goldridge_College&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Sport")]

Students are expected to take part in at least one sport each term. Goldridge College offers numerous sports such as [athletics](/wiki/Track_and_field "Track and field"), [basketball](/wiki/Basketball "Basketball"), [cross country](/wiki/Cross_country_running "Cross country running"), [cricket](/wiki/Cricket "Cricket"), [golf](/wiki/Golf "Golf"),[[5]](#cite_note-5) [hockey](/wiki/Field_hockey "Field hockey"), [netball](/wiki/Netball "Netball"), [rugby](/wiki/Rugby_football "Rugby football"), [soccer](/wiki/Association_football "Association football"), [swimming](/wiki/Swimming_(sport) "Swimming (sport)"), and [tennis](/wiki/Tennis "Tennis").

## Clubs and Cultural Activities

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Goldridge_College&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Clubs and Cultural Activities")]

Goldridge College offers the following cultural activities: Drama, Debating, Quiz, Public Speaking, Elocution, Choir, Marimba band and Jazz band.

Goldridge College students are required to attend at least one club activity. The clubs offered include Youth Alive, Chess, Interact, Library, Debate and Public Speaking, Information Communication Technology and Media, Grooming, [Interact](/wiki/Interact_Club "Interact Club"), [Leo Club](/wiki/Leo_clubs "Leo clubs"), Instrumental Music, Scripture Union, Art, SETA Engineering,Junior Achievers, Young Artists Poetry Society, and [Mathematical olympiad](/wiki/Mathematical_olympiad "Mathematical olympiad").

## Alumni

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Goldridge_College&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Alumni")]

Most of Goldridge College's alumni go on to further their education at tertiary level. Some alumni have been accepted to prestigious universities such as [Columbia University in the City of New York](/wiki/Columbia_University "Columbia University"), [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") and the [University of Cape Town](/wiki/University_of_Cape_Town "University of Cape Town").

Alumni who have attended Goldridge Primary School and Goldridge College are usually referred to as 'Goldridgians'.
