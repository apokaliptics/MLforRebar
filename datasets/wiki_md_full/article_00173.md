Musical romantic comedy television series

***Up Here*** is an American [musical](/wiki/Musical_film "Musical film") [romantic comedy](/wiki/Romantic_comedy "Romantic comedy") television series developed by [Steven Levenson](/wiki/Steven_Levenson "Steven Levenson"), [Kristen Anderson-Lopez](/wiki/Kristen_Anderson-Lopez "Kristen Anderson-Lopez"), [Robert Lopez](/wiki/Robert_Lopez "Robert Lopez"), and Danielle Sanchez-Witzel for [Hulu](/wiki/Hulu "Hulu") that premiered on March 24, 2023. In July 2023, the series was canceled after one season.

## Production

### Development

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Up_Here_(TV_series)&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Development")]

The series was based on the stage musical of the same name by Lopez and Anderson-Lopez, which played at the [La Jolla Playhouse](/wiki/La_Jolla_Playhouse "La Jolla Playhouse") in 2015.[[1]](#cite_note-1) The series was first announced in January 2022 when [Hulu](/wiki/Hulu "Hulu") gave the project a series order. [Steven Levenson](/wiki/Steven_Levenson "Steven Levenson"), [Kristen Anderson-Lopez](/wiki/Kristen_Anderson-Lopez "Kristen Anderson-Lopez"), [Robert Lopez](/wiki/Robert_Lopez "Robert Lopez"), and Danielle Sanchez-Witzel were set as writers and executive producers.[[2]](#cite_note-2) On July 28, 2023, Hulu canceled the series after one season.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

### Casting

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Up_Here_(TV_series)&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Casting")]

[Mae Whitman](/wiki/Mae_Whitman "Mae Whitman") was cast in March 2022, and [Carlos Valdes](/wiki/Carlos_Valdes_(actor) "Carlos Valdes (actor)") was cast in June.[[4]](#cite_note-4)[[5]](#cite_note-5) Later that month, [Katie Finneran](/wiki/Katie_Finneran "Katie Finneran"), [John Hodgman](/wiki/John_Hodgman "John Hodgman"), [Andréa Burns](/wiki/Andr%C3%A9a_Burns "Andréa Burns"), Sophia Hammons, and Emilia Suárez were cast as series regulars, along with George Hampe, Julia McDermott, [Scott Porter](/wiki/Scott_Porter "Scott Porter"), Ayumi Patterson, and [Brian Stokes Mitchell](/wiki/Brian_Stokes_Mitchell "Brian Stokes Mitchell") in recurring roles.[[6]](#cite_note-6)

## Release

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Up_Here_(TV_series)&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Release")]

All eight episodes were released on [Hulu](/wiki/Hulu "Hulu"), and internationally on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") (via [Star](/wiki/Star_(Disney%2B) "Star (Disney+)")) and [Star+](/wiki/Star%2B "Star+") in the Latin America,[[7]](#cite_note-7) on March 24, 2023.[[8]](#cite_note-8)

## Reception

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Up_Here_(TV_series)&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Reception")]

The [review aggregator](/wiki/Review_aggregator "Review aggregator") website [Rotten Tomatoes](/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes "Rotten Tomatoes") reported a 53% approval rating with an average rating of 6.2/10, based on 17 critic reviews. The website's critics consensus reads, "[Mae Whitman](/wiki/Mae_Whitman "Mae Whitman") and [Carlos Valdes](/wiki/Carlos_Valdes_(actor) "Carlos Valdes (actor)") are a likable enough pair, but *Up Here* is let down by a thin story that knocks its melody off-key."[[9]](#cite_note-9) [Metacritic](/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic"), which uses a weighted average, assigned a score of 53 out of 100 based on 6 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".[[10]](#cite_note-10)
