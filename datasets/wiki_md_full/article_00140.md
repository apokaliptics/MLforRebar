Public university in Loja

The **National University of Loja** ([Spanish](/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language"): *Universidad Nacional de Loja*) is a [public university](/wiki/Public_university "Public university") in [Loja](/wiki/Loja,_Ecuador "Loja, Ecuador"), southern [Ecuador](/wiki/Ecuador "Ecuador"). University-level teaching in the city dates to 1859, when the Federal Government of Loja, led by [Manuel Carrión Pinzano](/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Carri%C3%B3n_Pinzano&action=edit&redlink=1 "Manuel Carrión Pinzano (page does not exist)"), ordered higher studies to be offered under the auspices of the then Colegio San Bernardo; in practice, Jurisprudence was the first effective chair.[[9]](#cite_note-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10) The university is considered one of the country’s oldest institutions of higher education.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=National_University_of_Loja&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

In 1859, during the Federal Government of Loja headed by Manuel Carrión Pinzano, a decree provided for higher studies (Jurisprudence, Philosophy and Letters, Theology, and Medicine) to be attached to Colegio San Bernardo; Jurisprudence effectively began that year, marking the start of higher education in Loja.[[12]](#cite_note-12)[[13]](#cite_note-13)[[14]](#cite_note-14)

On 26 December 1895, Supreme Chief [Eloy Alfaro](/wiki/Eloy_Alfaro "Eloy Alfaro") issued a decree creating the Faculty of Jurisprudence in Loja, attached to Colegio San Bernardo, and authorizing it to confer academic degrees in law—consolidating the city’s university tradition.[[15]](#cite_note-15)[[16]](#cite_note-16)

By executive decree published in the *Registro Oficial* No. 948 (27 October 1943), the existing university board in Loja was elevated to the category of *Universidad*, enabling the new National University of Loja to confer academic degrees.[[17]](#cite_note-17)[[18]](#cite_note-18)

## Organization

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=National_University_of_Loja&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Organization")]

UNL operates as a public, secular university under Ecuadorian higher education law and maintains its main campus at the *Ciudad Universitaria Guillermo Falconí Espinosa* in Loja. Official planning and accountability instruments describe its academic structure and programs (primary sources for current data).[[19]](#cite_note-19)
