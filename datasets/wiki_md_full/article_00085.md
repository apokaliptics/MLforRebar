American basketball player (born 1998)

**Isaiah Maurice Livers**  ([/ˈlɪvərz/](/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [*LIH-vərz*](/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key");[[1]](#cite_note-1) born July 28, 1998) is an American professional [basketball](/wiki/Basketball "Basketball") player for the [Phoenix Suns](/wiki/Phoenix_Suns "Phoenix Suns") of the [National Basketball Association](/wiki/National_Basketball_Association "National Basketball Association") (NBA), on a two-way contract with the [Valley Suns](/wiki/Valley_Suns "Valley Suns") of the [NBA G League](/wiki/NBA_G_League "NBA G League"). He played [college basketball](/wiki/College_basketball "College basketball") for the [Michigan Wolverines](/wiki/Michigan_Wolverines_men%27s_basketball "Michigan Wolverines men's basketball"). He attended [Kalamazoo Central High School](/wiki/Kalamazoo_Central_High_School "Kalamazoo Central High School") where he won the [Mr. Basketball of Michigan](/wiki/Mr._Basketball_of_Michigan "Mr. Basketball of Michigan"). He was part of the [2017–18 team](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Michigan_Wolverines_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Michigan Wolverines men's basketball team") that won the [2018 Big Ten Conference men's basketball tournament](/wiki/2018_Big_Ten_Conference_men%27s_basketball_tournament "2018 Big Ten Conference men's basketball tournament") and reached the [championship game](/wiki/2018_NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Basketball_Championship_Game "2018 NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Championship Game") of the [2018 NCAA Division I men's basketball tournament](/wiki/2018_NCAA_Division_I_men%27s_basketball_tournament "2018 NCAA Division I men's basketball tournament").

## Early life

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life")]

Livers was born on July 28, 1998, in [Kalamazoo, Michigan](/wiki/Kalamazoo,_Michigan "Kalamazoo, Michigan"), to Angela and Morris Livers.[[2]](#cite_note-2) He started playing basketball at the age of five with his father in the driveway.[[3]](#cite_note-3) Michigan began recruiting Livers in May 2016 and he made his official visit on July 28, earning an offer.[[4]](#cite_note-4) At the end of his recruitment, he was considering visiting [Cal](/wiki/California_Golden_Bears_men%27s_basketball "California Golden Bears men's basketball"), but decided to cancel the visit and commit to Michigan.[[5]](#cite_note-5) On August 7, 2016, Livers committed to Michigan over contenders Michigan State, Butler, Minnesota, California, and Boston College.[[6]](#cite_note-6)[[7]](#cite_note-7) Livers signed his [National Letter of Intent](/wiki/National_Letter_of_Intent "National Letter of Intent") to Michigan as part of a three-scholarship player incoming class with [Jordan Poole](/wiki/Jordan_Poole "Jordan Poole") and Eli Brooks.[[8]](#cite_note-8)

He was named 2017 Michigan [Gatorade Player of the Year](/wiki/Gatorade_Basketball_Player_of_the_Year "Gatorade Basketball Player of the Year") and [Mr. Basketball of Michigan](/wiki/Mr._Basketball_of_Michigan "Mr. Basketball of Michigan").[[9]](#cite_note-9) Livers edged out second-place finisher [Xavier Tillman](/wiki/Xavier_Tillman "Xavier Tillman") by a 2,811–2,739 vote margin, although Tillman received three more first-place ballots.[[10]](#cite_note-det-10) It was the closest vote in Mr. Basketball of Michigan history.[[10]](#cite_note-det-10) Livers was Michigan's 11th Mr. Basketball of Michigan, but the first since [Manny Harris](/wiki/Manny_Harris "Manny Harris") in 2007.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

## College career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: College career")]

### Freshman season (2017–2018)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Freshman season (2017–2018)")]

On January 2, 2018, Michigan defeated [Iowa](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Iowa_Hawkeyes_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Iowa Hawkeyes men's basketball team") 75–68 with Livers contributing a career-best 13 points,[[12]](#cite_note-12)[[13]](#cite_note-13) which was considered a breakout performance.[[14]](#cite_note-14) It was the first of three consecutive double-digit efforts off the bench for Livers.[[15]](#cite_note-15) This resulted in Livers replacing [Duncan Robinson](/wiki/Duncan_Robinson_(basketball) "Duncan Robinson (basketball)") in the starting lineup for Michigan when they faced [Michigan State](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Michigan_State_Spartans_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Michigan State Spartans men's basketball team") in their [rivalry game](/wiki/Michigan%E2%80%93Michigan_State_men%27s_basketball_rivalry "Michigan–Michigan State men's basketball rivalry") on January 13.[[16]](#cite_note-16)[[17]](#cite_note-17) Michigan defeated the fourth-ranked Spartans for their first victory on the road against a top-five ranked team since January 25, 2014,[[18]](#cite_note-18)[[19]](#cite_note-19) ushering their way into the [2017–18 basketball rankings](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_NCAA_Division_I_men%27s_basketball_rankings "2017–18 NCAA Division I men's basketball rankings").[[20]](#cite_note-20) Livers rolled his ankle in the second minute of the game against [Northwestern](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Northwestern_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Northwestern Wildcats men's basketball team") on February 6 sidelining him for the rest of the game, which Michigan went on to lose.[[21]](#cite_note-21) He missed the next game against [Wisconsin](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Wisconsin_Badgers_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Wisconsin Badgers men's basketball team") before returning to the starting lineup on February 14 against [Iowa](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Iowa_Hawkeyes_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Iowa Hawkeyes men's basketball team").[[22]](#cite_note-22)[[23]](#cite_note-23) The team lost in the [2018 NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Championship Game](/wiki/2018_NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Basketball_Championship_Game "2018 NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Championship Game") to second-ranked [Villanova](/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Villanova_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball_team "2017–18 Villanova Wildcats men's basketball team").[[24]](#cite_note-24)[[25]](#cite_note-25)

### Sophomore season (2018–2019)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Sophomore season (2018–2019)")]

On January 13, [the 2018–19 Wolverines team](/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Michigan_Wolverines_men%27s_basketball_team "2018–19 Michigan Wolverines men's basketball team") defeated [Northwestern](/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Northwestern_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball_team "2018–19 Northwestern Wildcats men's basketball team") to establish a school record for best start at 17–0 and tied the school's record 17-game win streak.[[26]](#cite_note-26)[[27]](#cite_note-27) On February 28, 2019, Michigan defeated [Nebraska](/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Nebraska_Cornhuskers_men%27s_basketball_team "2018–19 Nebraska Cornhuskers men's basketball team") 82–53. Livers made his first start of the season, replacing an injured [Charles Matthews](/wiki/Charles_Matthews_(basketball) "Charles Matthews (basketball)"), and posted 12 points and 10 assists, for his first career double-double.[[28]](#cite_note-28)[[29]](#cite_note-29) On March 14, Michigan defeated [Minnesota](/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Minnesota_Golden_Gophers_men%27s_basketball_team "2018–19 Minnesota Golden Gophers men's basketball team") 76–49 in the semifinals of the Big Ten tournament, as Livers posted a career-high 21 points.[[30]](#cite_note-30)[[31]](#cite_note-31) Livers' 42.6% (52/122) three point shooting percentage led the Big Ten Conference.[[32]](#cite_note-32)

### Junior season (2019–2020)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Junior season (2019–2020)")]

On November 12, Michigan defeated [Creighton](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Creighton_Bluejays_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Creighton Bluejays men's basketball team") 79–69 in the [Gavitt Tipoff Games](/wiki/Gavitt_Tipoff_Games "Gavitt Tipoff Games") behind a then career-high 22 points from Livers.[[33]](#cite_note-33)[[34]](#cite_note-34) On November 22, Michigan defeated [Houston Baptist](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Houston_Baptist_Huskies_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Houston Baptist Huskies men's basketball team") 111–68 behind a career-high 24 points by Livers. Michigan's 111 points were the most points scored in a game since a 112–64 victory over [Indiana](/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_Indiana_Hoosiers_men%27s_basketball_team "1997–98 Indiana Hoosiers men's basketball team") in [1998](/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_Michigan_Wolverines_men%27s_basketball_team "1997–98 Michigan Wolverines men's basketball team").[[35]](#cite_note-35)[[36]](#cite_note-36) He suffered a groin injury on December 21 in a win over [Presbyterian](/wiki/Presbyterian_Blue_Hose_men%27s_basketball "Presbyterian Blue Hose men's basketball").[[37]](#cite_note-37) He missed nine of the next 10 games before facing [Michigan State](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Michigan_State_Spartans_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Michigan State Spartans men's basketball team") on February 8.[[38]](#cite_note-38) On February 16, Livers suffered an ankle injury against [Indiana](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Indiana_Hoosiers_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Indiana Hoosiers men's basketball team") and missed the following game against [Rutgers](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Rutgers_Scarlet_Knights_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Rutgers Scarlet Knights men's basketball team"). In his return versus [Purdue](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Purdue_Boilermakers_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Purdue Boilermakers men's basketball team"), Livers had 19 points, six rebounds, two blocks and a steal in a 71–63 victory.[[39]](#cite_note-39) On March 5, Michigan defeated [Nebraska](/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Nebraska_Cornhuskers_men%27s_basketball_team "2019–20 Nebraska Cornhuskers men's basketball team") 82–58, as Livers posted 18 points and ten rebounds, for his second career double-double.[[40]](#cite_note-40)[[41]](#cite_note-41) At the close of the regular season, Livers was named Honorable Mention All-Big Ten by the media.[[42]](#cite_note-42) Livers started 21 games and averaged 12.9 points per game while shooting 40 percent from behind the arc. He missed only two free-throws the entire season, making 44-for-46 (.957) from the line.[[43]](#cite_note-43)

Following the season, he declared for the [2020 NBA draft](/wiki/2020_NBA_draft "2020 NBA draft"), but did not hire an agent and left the door open to return for his senior season.[[44]](#cite_note-44) On July 17, 2020, Livers announced that he would return to Michigan for his senior season.[[45]](#cite_note-45)

### Senior season (2020–2021)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Senior season (2020–2021)")]

During his senior season, Livers posted 16 double-figure games with a team-high five 20+ point games, and averaged 13.9 points per game. Livers made a team-high 50 three-pointers, reaching 50+ in a season for the second time in his career. He averaged a career-best 6.2 rebounds and 2.0 assists, and he added a pair of double-doubles. Following the season, he was named second-team All-Big Ten by the coaches and media.[[46]](#cite_note-46)[[47]](#cite_note-47) On March 13, 2021, Livers suffered a stress injury to his right foot during a quarterfinal game against [Maryland](/wiki/Maryland_Terrapins_men%27s_basketball "Maryland Terrapins men's basketball") in the [2021 Big Ten tournament](/wiki/2021_Big_Ten_men%27s_basketball_tournament "2021 Big Ten men's basketball tournament"), which sidelined him indefinitely.[[48]](#cite_note-48) On April 2, 2021, Livers underwent successful surgery on his foot, with his recovery time expected to be a minimum of six months.[[49]](#cite_note-49) On April 16, 2021, Livers declared for the [2021 NBA draft](/wiki/2021_NBA_draft "2021 NBA draft").[[50]](#cite_note-50)

## Professional career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Professional career")]

### Detroit Pistons (2021–2024)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Detroit Pistons (2021–2024)")]

Livers was selected in the second round of the [2021 NBA draft](/wiki/2021_NBA_draft "2021 NBA draft") with the 42nd pick by his hometown team the [Detroit Pistons](/wiki/Detroit_Pistons "Detroit Pistons").[[51]](#cite_note-51) On August 8, he signed with the Pistons.[[52]](#cite_note-CunninghamLivers-52) Because of a foot injury, he missed 59 of the first 60 games during his rookie season.[[53]](#cite_note-53) On March 15, 2022, in his tenth game, Livers went 4–5 on three point shots to post a career high 16 points against the [Miami Heat](/wiki/2021-22_Miami_Heat_season "2021-22 Miami Heat season"),[[54]](#cite_note-54) as Livers was in the rotation in place of the injured [Cade Cunningham](/wiki/Cade_Cunningham "Cade Cunningham"), while [Frank Jackson](/wiki/Frank_Jackson_(basketball) "Frank Jackson (basketball)") and [Hamidou Diallo](/wiki/Hamidou_Diallo "Hamidou Diallo") were sidelined to injuries.[[55]](#cite_note-55) In his first NBA start on April 1, he posted career highs of 17 points and 11 rebounds for his first double-double against [Oklahoma City](/wiki/2021-22_Oklahoma_City_Thunder_season "2021-22 Oklahoma City Thunder season").[[56]](#cite_note-56)

On March 11, 2023, Livers posted a career high 18 points against the [Indiana Pacers](/wiki/2022-23_Indiana_Pacers_season "2022-23 Indiana Pacers season").[[57]](#cite_note-57) On June 29, 2023, the [Detroit Pistons](/wiki/Detroit_Pistons "Detroit Pistons") exercised Livers’ team option for the [2023–24 NBA season](/wiki/2023%E2%80%9324_NBA_season "2023–24 NBA season").[[58]](#cite_note-58)

On January 14, 2024, Livers was traded to the [Washington Wizards](/wiki/Washington_Wizards "Washington Wizards"), along with [Marvin Bagley III](/wiki/Marvin_Bagley_III "Marvin Bagley III") and future draft considerations in exchange for [Danilo Gallinari](/wiki/Danilo_Gallinari "Danilo Gallinari") and [Mike Muscala](/wiki/Mike_Muscala "Mike Muscala").[[59]](#cite_note-59) On February 26, 2024, the [Washington Wizards](/wiki/Washington_Wizards "Washington Wizards") announced that Livers would miss the remainder of [2023–24 NBA season](/wiki/2023%E2%80%9324_NBA_season "2023–24 NBA season") due to joint capsule inflammation in his right hip.[[60]](#cite_note-60) However, he was waived by the Wizards on April 5 before playing for them.[[61]](#cite_note-61) On October 18, 2024, Livers underwent a hip resurfacing procedure that was seen as likely to take a full year to recover from.[[62]](#cite_note-62)

### Phoenix Suns (2025–present)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Isaiah_Livers&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Phoenix Suns (2025–present)")]

On July 8, 2025, Livers signed a [two-way contract](/wiki/Two-way_contract "Two-way contract") with the [Phoenix Suns](/wiki/Phoenix_Suns "Phoenix Suns").[[63]](#cite_note-63)
