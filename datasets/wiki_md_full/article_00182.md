Neighourhood in British Columbia, Canada

Neighbourhood of Burnaby in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada

**Edmonds** is a neighbourhood in the southeast of [Burnaby](/wiki/Burnaby "Burnaby"), British Columbia, Canada. It is one of the city's four officially designated town centres.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

The area is served by the [Expo Line](/wiki/Expo_Line_(SkyTrain) "Expo Line (SkyTrain)") of the [SkyTrain](/wiki/SkyTrain_(Vancouver) "SkyTrain (Vancouver)") via [Edmonds](/wiki/Edmonds_station_(SkyTrain) "Edmonds station (SkyTrain)") station.

The neighbourhood is named after businessman and politician [Henry Valentine Edmonds](/w/index.php?title=Henry_Valentine_Edmonds&action=edit&redlink=1 "Henry Valentine Edmonds (page does not exist)"), who owned real estate in the area and was active in local affairs.[[8]](#cite_note-8)

## Features and amenities

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Edmonds,_Burnaby&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Features and amenities")]

Edmonds is home to the Rosemary Brown Recreation Centre, which holds two [NHL](/wiki/National_Hockey_League "National Hockey League")-sized [ice hockey](/wiki/Ice_hockey "Ice hockey") rinks and is named after local politician [Rosemary Brown](/wiki/Rosemary_Brown_(Canadian_politician) "Rosemary Brown (Canadian politician)").[[9]](#cite_note-9)

Public schools in the area are operated by the [Burnaby School District](/wiki/School_District_41_Burnaby "School District 41 Burnaby"). Twelfth Avenue Elementary School and Edmonds Community Elementary School both fall within Edmonds' borders, as well as the private Catholic elementary school Our Lady of Mercy. The secondary schools serving Edmonds are [Byrne Creek Community School](/wiki/Byrne_Creek_Community_School "Byrne Creek Community School") and the independent private school [St. Thomas More Collegiate](/wiki/St._Thomas_More_Collegiate "St. Thomas More Collegiate").

## Demographics

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Edmonds,_Burnaby&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Demographics")]

With numerous ethnic backgrounds and over a hundred languages accounted for, Edmonds was found by the *[Vancouver Sun](/wiki/Vancouver_Sun "Vancouver Sun")* to be the most diverse neighbourhood in [Metro Vancouver](/wiki/Metro_Vancouver_Regional_District "Metro Vancouver Regional District").[[10]](#cite_note-10)
