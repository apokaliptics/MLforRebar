Australian radiologist

**Sir Clive Wentworth Uhr** [CBE](/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire "Order of the British Empire") (1 January 1903 – 19 September 1974) was an Australian [radiologist](/wiki/Radiologist "Radiologist").[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1) Born in [Brisbane](/wiki/Brisbane "Brisbane") to a family of Australian-Irish ancestry, grandson of prospector [Wentworth D'Arcy Uhr](/wiki/Wentworth_D%27Arcy_Uhr "Wentworth D'Arcy Uhr"), Uhr was educated at the [University of Sydney](/wiki/University_of_Sydney "University of Sydney"). Following graduation he pursued a medical career at the [Royal Brisbane Hospital](/wiki/Royal_Brisbane_Hospital "Royal Brisbane Hospital") before joining private practice in 1932, and then the [Australian Army Medical Corps](/wiki/Australian_Army_Medical_Corps "Australian Army Medical Corps") in 1940.[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1)

Quickly promoted to major, Uhr was ordered to [Singapore](/wiki/Singapore "Singapore") and was on the day of the [Japanese invasion of Singapore](/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Singapore "Japanese invasion of Singapore") during [World War II](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II").[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1) Uhr, an anaesthetist and radiologist during the campaign, was captured. While a prisoner he continued to provide medical care for fellow prisoners with [cardiac beriberi](/wiki/Beriberi "Beriberi"), remaining a [prisoner of war](/wiki/Prisoner_of_war "Prisoner of war") until the end of hostilities in 1945.[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1) In peacetime Uhr remained a staunch supporter of war veterans, marching for the Catholic War Veterans' Association on [Anzac Day](/wiki/Anzac_Day "Anzac Day"). He founded, and was a member of, several medical boards in [Queensland](/wiki/Queensland "Queensland"), including a local branch of the Australian and New Zealand Association of Radiology.[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1)

Uhr also entered the world of [horse racing](/wiki/Horse_racing "Horse racing"), and was chairman of a Queensland racing club. He also donated to colleges of the [University of Queensland](/wiki/University_of_Queensland "University of Queensland") and stood in local elections. He was made [Commander of the Order of the British Empire](/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire "Commander of the Order of the British Empire") in 1961 and [knighted](/wiki/Knighted "Knighted") in 1972.[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1)

Uhr died in 1974 and was buried in [Nudgee Cemetery](/wiki/Nudgee_Cemetery "Nudgee Cemetery").[[1]](#cite_note-ADB-1)
