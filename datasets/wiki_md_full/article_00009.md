Australian botanist, arborist and ecologist

**Dean Nicolle** (born 1974), is an Australian [botanist](/wiki/Botanist "Botanist"), [arborist](/wiki/Arboriculture "Arboriculture"), and [ecologist](/wiki/Ecologist "Ecologist"). He is widely recognised as the leading authority on the genus *[Eucalyptus](/wiki/Eucalyptus "Eucalyptus")*.

## Early life and education

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dean_Nicolle&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")]

Dean Nicolle was born in [Adelaide, South Australia](/wiki/Adelaide,_South_Australia "Adelaide, South Australia"), in 1974.[[1]](#cite_note-ANH-1)

He developed an interest in *Eucalyptus* trees as a young man.[[1]](#cite_note-ANH-1) After completing a Bachelor of Science at the [University of Adelaide](/wiki/University_of_Adelaide "University of Adelaide"),[[2]](#cite_note-2) he went on to complete his [PhD](/wiki/PhD "PhD") in 2008 on [mallee trees](/wiki/Mallee_(habit) "Mallee (habit)") at [Flinders University](/wiki/Flinders_University "Flinders University").[[3]](#cite_note-3)

## Career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dean_Nicolle&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Career")]

Nicolle is a botanist, arborist, and ecologist.[[1]](#cite_note-ANH-1) He is widely recognised as the leading authority on the genus *[Eucalyptus](/wiki/Eucalyptus "Eucalyptus")*.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Nicolle created the [Currency Creek Arboretum](/wiki/Currency_Creek_Arboretum "Currency Creek Arboretum"), on a 30 hectares (74 acres) site in the [Fleurieu region](/wiki/Fleurieu_and_Kangaroo_Island "Fleurieu and Kangaroo Island") in [South Australia](/wiki/South_Australia "South Australia").

As of 2017[[update]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dean_Nicolle&action=edit) he is the director and head of research at the arboretum. He has cultivated over 900 species and subspecies of *Eucalyptus*.[[4]](#cite_note-4)

## Recognition

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dean_Nicolle&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Recognition")]

Nicolle was awarded the [Centenary Medal](/wiki/Centenary_Medal "Centenary Medal") in 2001[[5]](#cite_note-5) and the Medal of the Order of Australia in the [2018 Queen's Birthday Honours](/wiki/2018_Queen%27s_Birthday_Honours_(Australia) "2018 Queen's Birthday Honours (Australia)") for "service to the conservation of Australian eucalypts".[[6]](#cite_note-6)

In August 2025, the 4.4-kilometre (2.7Â mi) Dean Nicolle Eucalypt Walk was opened along a section of the old railway corridor at [Blyth, South Australia](/wiki/Blyth,_South_Australia "Blyth, South Australia"). The walk is the brainchild of artist [Ian Roberts](/wiki/Ian_Roberts_(painter) "Ian Roberts (painter)"), who named it after Nicolle, following a long partnership whereby Roberts painted watercolours of eucalypts that he grew from seeds provided by Nicolle. As of September 2025, Roberts had painted around 770 of the 900 species of eucalypts. The trail features more than 2,000 Australian plant species, including 300 varieties of rare eucalypt, planted by volunteers between 2010 and 2019.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

The standard [author abbreviation](/wiki/List_of_botanists_by_author_abbreviation_(A) "List of botanists by author abbreviation (A)") D.Nicolle is used to indicate this person as the author when [citing](/wiki/Author_citation_(botany) "Author citation (botany)") a [botanical name](/wiki/Botanical_name "Botanical name").[[8]](#cite_note-8)
