Historic meetinghouse in New Jersey, United States

United States historic place

**Randolph Friends Meetinghouse** is a historic Quaker [meeting house](/wiki/Friends_meeting_house "Friends meeting house") in [Randolph](/wiki/Randolph,_New_Jersey "Randolph, New Jersey"), [Morris County](/wiki/Morris_County,_New_Jersey "Morris County, New Jersey"), [New Jersey](/wiki/New_Jersey "New Jersey"), United States. The land on which the Meeting House stands was purchased by a small group of [Quakers](/wiki/Quakers "Quakers") in 1758 for a burying ground, i.e. [Cemetery](/wiki/Cemetery "Cemetery") The same year a subscription was raised to build a [Meeting House](/wiki/Meeting_house "Meeting house") on the site for religious and business meeting.

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Friends_Meetinghouse_(Randolph,_New_Jersey)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

Presumably, [English colonists](/wiki/Colonial_history_of_the_United_States "Colonial history of the United States") built the Meetinghouse, completing it in 1758.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

On October 22, 1898, a board of [trustees](/wiki/Trustee "Trustee") formed the Friends Meeting House and Cemetery Association and bought the site in order to preserve it for future generations.[[4]](#cite_note-4)[[5]](#cite_note-5)

The site was added to the [National Register of Historic Places](/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places "National Register of Historic Places") in 1973.[[6]](#cite_note-nrhpnom-6)
