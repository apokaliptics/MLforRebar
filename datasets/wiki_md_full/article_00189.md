Species of lichen

***Diorygma incantatum*** is a species of [corticolous](/wiki/Corticolous_lichen "Corticolous lichen") (bark-dwelling) [crustose lichen](/wiki/Crustose_lichen "Crustose lichen") in the family [Graphidaceae](/wiki/Graphidaceae "Graphidaceae"). It is found in northern and southern Brazil.

## Taxonomy

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Diorygma_incantatum&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Taxonomy")]

The lichen was [formally described](/wiki/Species_description "Species description") as new to science in 2014 by Shirley Cunha Feuerstein and Sionara Eliasaro. The [type specimen](/wiki/Type_(biology) "Type (biology)") was collected by the first author in the Encantadas of [Ilha do Mel State Park](/wiki/Ilha_do_Mel_State_Park "Ilha do Mel State Park") ([Paraná](/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_(state) "Paraná (state)")), where it was found growing on tree bark in a [Restinga](/wiki/Restinga "Restinga") forest. The [species epithet](/wiki/Botanical_name "Botanical name") *incantatum* ([Latin](/wiki/Latin "Latin") for "enchanted") alludes to the [type locality](/wiki/Type_(biology)#type_locality "Type (biology)"), Encantadas ([Portuguese](/wiki/Portuguese_language "Portuguese language") for "enchanted"), which refers to this area on the Ilha do Mel.[[1]](#cite_note-Feuerstein_et_al._2014-1) The range of the species was later extended to northern Brazil when, in 2017, it was recorded near [Itaguatins](/wiki/Itaguatins "Itaguatins") in the [state](/wiki/Federative_units_of_Brazil "Federative units of Brazil") of [Tocantins](/wiki/Tocantins_(state) "Tocantins (state)").[[2]](#cite_note-Aptroot_et_al._2017-2)

## Description

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Diorygma_incantatum&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Description")]

*Diorygma incantatum* grows as an opaque, irregular, whitish grey crust on the bark substrate. It lacks [soredia](/wiki/Soredia "Soredia") and [isidia](/wiki/Isidia "Isidia") (vegetative [propagules](/wiki/Propagule "Propagule")). Its [ascomata](/wiki/Ascomata "Ascomata") are white, have a rounded to irregular shape, and measure 0.6–1.6 by 0.3–0.6 mm; they have a pinkish disc with white [pruina](/wiki/Pruina "Pruina"). The major distinguishing characteristic of the lichen are its threadlike (*filiform*) [ascospores](/wiki/Ascospore "Ascospore"), which measure 105–108 by 6 [μm](/wiki/Micrometre "Micrometre") and have from 29 to 31 transverse [septa](/wiki/Septum "Septum").[[1]](#cite_note-Feuerstein_et_al._2014-1)

At the time of publication, only four other *[Diorygma](/wiki/Diorygma "Diorygma")* species were known to have transverse septa in their ascospores. These lichens (*[D. circumfusum](/w/index.php?title=Diorygma_circumfusum&action=edit&redlink=1 "Diorygma circumfusum (page does not exist)")*, *[D. minisporum](/wiki/Diorygma_minisporum "Diorygma minisporum")*, *[D. wallamanense](/w/index.php?title=Diorygma_wallamanense&action=edit&redlink=1 "Diorygma wallamanense (page does not exist)")*, and *[D. wilsonianum](/w/index.php?title=Diorygma_wilsonianum&action=edit&redlink=1 "Diorygma wilsonianum (page does not exist)")* ) can be distinguished from *D. incantatum* by differences in distribution and in spore dimensions and structure. Additionally, *D. incantatum* contains an unknown [lichen product](/wiki/Lichen_product "Lichen product") that forms a purple spot when analysed and separated with [thin-layer chromatography](/wiki/Thin-layer_chromatography "Thin-layer chromatography"); this spot will [fluoresce](/wiki/Fluorescence "Fluorescence") orange when lit with an [ultraviolet](/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet") light. The authors also noted a resemblance with *[Chapsa indica](/w/index.php?title=Chapsa_indica&action=edit&redlink=1 "Chapsa indica (page does not exist)")*, a species which differs from *D. incantatum* in that it contains periphysoids, and it lacks lichen products.[[1]](#cite_note-Feuerstein_et_al._2014-1)
