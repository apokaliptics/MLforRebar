Government ministry of Cuba

The **Ministry of the Interior of the Republic of Cuba** ([Spanish](/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language"): *Ministerio del Interior de la República de Cuba*), also known as **MININT**, is the [Cuban government](/wiki/Government_of_Cuba "Government of Cuba") [ministry](/wiki/Ministry_(government_department) "Ministry (government department)") which oversees the [home affairs](/wiki/Interior_ministry "Interior ministry") of [Cuba](/wiki/Cuba "Cuba"). Its headquarter is in a building of [Plaza de la Revolución](/wiki/Plaza_de_la_Revoluci%C3%B3n "Plaza de la Revolución"), a central and famous square of [Havana](/wiki/Havana "Havana").

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Cuba)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

It was founded on June 6, 1961, replacing and expanding the old Ministry of the Interior (*Ministerio de Gobernación*), inherited by the [Cuban Revolution](/wiki/Cuban_Revolution "Cuban Revolution") from the previous governments.[[2]](#cite_note-2)

## Functions

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Cuba)&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Functions")]

The organs and structures that are part of the Minister fulfill functions of citizen security, and the establishment of the internal order.[[3]](#cite_note-cubagov-3) The MININT also includes various logistics agencies, force preparation, etc. In addition, it has commercial companies that provide security services as SEPSA (Specialized Protection Services, S.A.), SEISA or ACERPROT), which include chain stores selling to the population.

The Ministry oversees the functions of security and public order through the [National Revolutionary Police Force](/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_Cuba "Law enforcement in Cuba") (PNR)[[4]](#cite_note-minint-struct-4) and the auxiliary body of the [Committees for the Defense of the Revolution](/wiki/Committees_for_the_Defense_of_the_Revolution "Committees for the Defense of the Revolution") (CDR).

The MININT has its own teaching system, with several national schools (higher institutes) and schools in the provinces. In [Havana](/wiki/Havana "Havana"), it has a polytechnic institute (named Instituto Superior del MININT Eliseo Reyes Rodríguez),[[4]](#cite_note-minint-struct-4) for the training of media technicians in specialties related to criminal and police investigation lines.

## Structure

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Cuba)&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Structure")]

The following structure is in place with MININT as of 2021:[[4]](#cite_note-minint-struct-4)

* [National Revolutionary Police Force](/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Police_Force "National Revolutionary Police Force")
* [Dirección de Inteligencia](/wiki/Direcci%C3%B3n_de_Inteligencia "Dirección de Inteligencia")
* Special National Brigade (or Black Berets)[[5]](#cite_note-5)[[6]](#cite_note-6)
* State Security Department
* Technical Directorate of Investigations
* Directorate of Border Guard Troops
* Cuban Fire Department
* Directorate of Personal Security
* Directorate of Criminal Investigation and Operations
* Directorate of Penitentiary Establishments
* Directorate of Attention to Minors
* Directorate of Identification, Immigration and Aliens

## Special National Brigade

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Cuba)&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Special National Brigade")]

The Special National Brigade ([Spanish](/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language"): *BRIGADA ESPECIAL NACIONAL DEL MINISTERIO DEL INTERIOR, BEN*), also referred to as the Black Berets ([Spanish](/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language"): *Boinas Negras*) or SNB, is a [special forces](/wiki/Special_forces "Special forces") [brigade](/wiki/Brigade "Brigade") run by the [Cuban Ministry of the Interior](/wiki/Cuban_Ministry_of_the_Interior "Cuban Ministry of the Interior") (MININT).[[7]](#cite_note-7) It performs missions that deal with [riot control](/wiki/Riot_control "Riot control"), [hostage rescue](/wiki/Hostage_rescue "Hostage rescue"), and [counterterrorism](/wiki/Counterterrorism "Counterterrorism").[[8]](#cite_note-8) The SNB was deployed in response to prodemocracy protests that began on July 11, 2021.[[9]](#cite_note-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10)[[11]](#cite_note-11) The United States government sanctioned them because of human rights concerns related to mass arrests and physical violence used against the protesters, which the Cuban government opposes.[[12]](#cite_note-12)[[13]](#cite_note-13)[[14]](#cite_note-14) The Black Berets celebrated the fortieth anniversary of its founding on August 9, 2020.[[15]](#cite_note-15)

As of April 4, 2022, the Special National Brigade is led by Brigadier General Noaldo Arcas Rodríguez.[[16]](#cite_note-16) The Black Berets have trained with [China](/wiki/China "China")'s [People’s Armed Police](/wiki/People%E2%80%99s_Armed_Police "People’s Armed Police") (PAP), who trained them in [hand-to-hand combat](/wiki/Hand-to-hand_combat "Hand-to-hand combat"), hostage rescue, tactical shooting, and handling large riots.
