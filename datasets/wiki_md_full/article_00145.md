Species of flowering plant

***Boronia ledifolia***, commonly known as the **Sydney boronia**, **showy boronia** or **ledum boronia**, is a plant in the citrus [family](/wiki/Family_(biology) "Family (biology)") [Rutaceae](/wiki/Rutaceae "Rutaceae") and is [endemic](/wiki/Endemism "Endemism") to south-eastern [Australia](/wiki/Australia "Australia"). It is a [shrub](/wiki/Shrub "Shrub") with simple or pinnate leaves which have a strong odour when crushed, and pale to bright pink flowers. Although difficult to propagate, this boronia is a popular garden plant.

## Description

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Boronia_ledifolia&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Description")]

*Boronia ledifolia* is a shrub which grows to a height of 0.3–2.5 m (1–8 ft) and has its thinnest branches covered with fine, matted hairs. The leaves are usually simple but may also be [pinnate](/wiki/Pinnation#Number_of_divisions "Pinnation") with from three to seven leaflets. The leaves or leaflets are narrow elliptic or oblong in shape, 3–43 mm (0.1–2 in) long and 1–7 mm (0.04–0.3 in) wide. They are dark green and [glabrous](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/glabrous "wikt:glabrous") on the upper surface and a lighter green with a thin layer of matted hairs on the lower side where there is a distinct mid-vein.[[2]](#cite_note-Harden-2)[[3]](#cite_note-plantnet-3)[[4]](#cite_note-vicflora-4)

The flowers are pale to bright pink, rarely white, and are arranged in groups of up to 35, but usually 3 to 6 in leaf [axils](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/axil "wikt:axil"). The groups are on a [peduncle](/wiki/Peduncle_(botany) "Peduncle (botany)") 1–10 mm (0.04–0.4 in) long, each flower on an individual stalk a further 6–11 mm (0.2–0.4 in) long. The [sepals](/wiki/Sepal "Sepal") are egg-shaped to triangular, 3–4.5 mm (0.1–0.2 in) long and the four petals are 5–12 mm (0.2–0.5 in) long. Flowering occurs in spring and early summer and is followed by the fruit which is a [follicle](/wiki/Follicle_(fruit) "Follicle (fruit)"), 4–5 mm (0.16–0.20 in) long.[[2]](#cite_note-Harden-2)[[3]](#cite_note-plantnet-3)[[4]](#cite_note-vicflora-4)

## Taxonomy and naming

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Boronia_ledifolia&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Taxonomy and naming")]

This species was first formally described in 1803 by the [French](/wiki/French_people "French people") [botanist](/wiki/Botanist "Botanist") [Étienne Pierre Ventenat](/wiki/%C3%89tienne_Pierre_Ventenat "Étienne Pierre Ventenat"), who gave it the name *Lasiopetalum ledifolium* and published the description in *Jardin de la Malmaison*.[[5]](#cite_note-APNI(1)-5)[[6]](#cite_note-Vent.-6) In 1824, [Augustin de Candolle](/wiki/Augustin_Pyramus_de_Candolle "Augustin Pyramus de Candolle") changed the name to *Boronia ledifolia* and published the name change in *[Prodromus Systematis Naturalis Regni Vegetabilis](/wiki/Prodromus_Systematis_Naturalis_Regni_Vegetabilis "Prodromus Systematis Naturalis Regni Vegetabilis")*.[[7]](#cite_note-APNI-7)[[8]](#cite_note-DC.-8) The [specific epithet](/wiki/Botanical_name "Botanical name") (*ledifolia*) refers to a perceived similarity to the leaves of plants in the genus [Ledum](/wiki/Rhododendron_subsect._Ledum "Rhododendron subsect. Ledum").[[9]](#cite_note-sutherland-9)

## Distribution and habitat

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Boronia_ledifolia&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Distribution and habitat")]

*Boronia ledifolia* mainly occurs south of [Scone](/wiki/Scone,_New_South_Wales "Scone, New South Wales") although there are [disjunct](/wiki/Disjunct_distribution "Disjunct distribution") populations in the [Torrington](/wiki/Torrington,_New_South_Wales "Torrington, New South Wales") and Bolivia Hill districts. It is rare in [Victoria](/wiki/Victoria_(Australia) "Victoria (Australia)") where it occurs in parts of the [Gippsland](/wiki/Gippsland "Gippsland") district. It usually grows in poor soils over sandstone or granite in heath and forest.[[2]](#cite_note-Harden-2)[[3]](#cite_note-plantnet-3)[[4]](#cite_note-vicflora-4)

## Cultivation

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Boronia_ledifolia&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Cultivation")]

[Boronias](/wiki/Boronia "Boronia") are mostly sensitive to [dieback](/wiki/Forest_dieback "Forest dieback") and tend to be short-lived in cultivation. This species needs a sunny, moist and well drained situation.[[10]](#cite_note-10)
