Annual Chinese film award

The **[Hong Kong Film Award](/wiki/Hong_Kong_Film_Award "Hong Kong Film Award")** for Best Cinematography is an annual [Hong Kong](/wiki/Hong_Kong_cinema "Hong Kong cinema") industry award presented to a [cinematographer](/wiki/Cinematographer "Cinematographer") or a group of cinematographers for the best achievement in [cinematography](/wiki/Cinematography "Cinematography").

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Hong_Kong_Film_Award_for_Best_Cinematography&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

The award was established at the [2nd Hong Kong Film Awards](/wiki/2nd_Hong_Kong_Film_Awards "2nd Hong Kong Film Awards") (1983) and the first winner in this category was [Arthur Wong](/wiki/Arthur_Wong "Arthur Wong") for his contribution in the cinematography of *[He Lives by Night](/w/index.php?title=He_Lives_by_Night&action=edit&redlink=1 "He Lives by Night (page does not exist)")*. Arthur Wong was also honoured at the [28th Hong Kong Film Awards](/wiki/28th_Hong_Kong_Film_Awards "28th Hong Kong Film Awards") (2009) for the film *[Painted Skin](/wiki/Painted_Skin_(2008_film) "Painted Skin (2008 film)")*. Arthur Wong holds the record for the most awards in this category with 8 wins.
