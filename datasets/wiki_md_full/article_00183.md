Australian violinist (1937–2013)

**Carmel Kaine** (22 March 1937 – 21 April 2013)[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) was an Australian classical [violinist](/wiki/Violin "Violin").

She was born in [Wagga Wagga, New South Wales](/wiki/Wagga_Wagga,_New_South_Wales "Wagga Wagga, New South Wales") and studied at the [New South Wales Conservatorium](/wiki/Sydney_Conservatorium_of_Music "Sydney Conservatorium of Music"), graduating at age 17 with the prize for the most outstanding student. Two years later, she spent a year as a member of the [South Australian Symphony Orchestra](/wiki/Adelaide_Symphony_Orchestra "Adelaide Symphony Orchestra").[[3]](#cite_note-3) She then continued her studies at the [Royal Academy of Music](/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Music "Royal Academy of Music") in [London](/wiki/London "London"), where she won the three violin prizes and the Violin Scholarship in her first year.[[4]](#cite_note-:0-4)

Kaine furthered her studies at the [Juilliard School](/wiki/Juilliard_School "Juilliard School") in [New York](/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") with [Ivan Galamian](/wiki/Ivan_Galamian "Ivan Galamian"). At the Juilliard School, Kaine was awarded a Violin Fellowship and in 1967 was awarded the first prize at the Vienna International Violin Competition. Recitals for the [BBC](/wiki/BBC "BBC") followed both as a soloist and in chamber ensembles. She was a member of the [Academy of St. Martin in the Fields](/wiki/Academy_of_St._Martin_in_the_Fields "Academy of St. Martin in the Fields") for ten years, making solo recordings with the academy and performing at major festivals throughout Europe.[[5]](#cite_note-5) Her recording of [Vivaldi](/wiki/Antonio_Vivaldi "Antonio Vivaldi")'s *[La stravaganza](/wiki/La_stravaganza "La stravaganza")*, Sir [Neville Marriner](/wiki/Neville_Marriner "Neville Marriner") conducting, won a [Grand Prix du Disque](/wiki/Grand_Prix_du_Disque "Grand Prix du Disque") and a Rosette Award in *[The Penguin Guide to Recorded Classical Music](/wiki/The_Penguin_Guide_to_Recorded_Classical_Music "The Penguin Guide to Recorded Classical Music")* ([Decca Records](/wiki/Decca_Records "Decca Records")425721).[[4]](#cite_note-:0-4)

Kaine was a professor at the Royal Academy of Music for twelve years and in 1983 was made a [Fellow](/wiki/Fellow "Fellow"). She was invited by [Yehudi Menuhin](/wiki/Yehudi_Menuhin "Yehudi Menuhin") to read at his school in [Cobham, Surrey](/wiki/Cobham,_Surrey "Cobham, Surrey"). In 1990 Kaine took up the Senior Lecturer position at the [Queensland Conservatorium Griffith University](/wiki/Queensland_Conservatorium_Griffith_University "Queensland Conservatorium Griffith University") and for five years was head of department.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

In 1991, with her husband John Willison, she founded the Limpinwood Ensemble and many performances have been given for the [ABC](/wiki/Australian_Broadcasting_Corporation "Australian Broadcasting Corporation") and at [Tyalgum](/wiki/Tyalgum,_New_South_Wales "Tyalgum, New South Wales") Classical Music Festival, which they also founded.[[6]](#cite_note-6) She also founded the Queensland Conservatorium Soloists, which has raised over $30,000 for the conservatorium's String Department.
