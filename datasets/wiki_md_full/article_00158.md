***Ranchers-Cattlemen Action Legal Fund v. USDA*** (No. 2:17-cv-00223) is a challenge to USDA rules that allow Mexican and Canadian beef to be labelled as domestic beef.[[1]](#cite_note-Docket_1-1)

This case is distinguished from ***[Ranchers-Cattlemen Action Legal Fund v. Sonny Perdue](/wiki/Ranchers-Cattlemen_Action_Legal_Fund_v._Sonny_Perdue "Ranchers-Cattlemen Action Legal Fund v. Sonny Perdue")*** (No. 4:16-cv-00041-BMM) in which plaintiffs allege that checkoff dollars are being used to support Canadian and Mexican beef.

## Facts and prior history

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Ranchers-Cattlemen_Action_Legal_Fund_v._USDA&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Facts and prior history")]

In 2016, the United States Department of Agriculture rescinded regulations requiring Mexican and Canadian beef be marked as imported. Plaintiffs filed the complaint May 19, 2017.[[1]](#cite_note-Docket_1-1)

In March 2018, plaintiffs asked for a summary judgement in their favor.[[2]](#cite_note-rcalf-2)
