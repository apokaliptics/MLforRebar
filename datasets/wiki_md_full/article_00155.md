American poet

**Louis Alemayehu** is an American poet, educator, musician, and community organizer.[[1]](#cite_note-MN_Voices-1)

## Biography

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Louis_Alemayehu&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Biography")]

Alemayehu was born December 31, 1945, in [Chicago](/wiki/Chicago "Chicago"), [Illinois](/wiki/Illinois "Illinois") to parents of African, American Indian, and European descent.[[1]](#cite_note-MN_Voices-1)

Alemayehu teaches Environmental Sustainability at the Higher Education Consortium for Urban Affairs in Minneapolis Minnesota. He has also been a community organizer with Environmental Justice Advocates of Minnesota (EJAM).[[2]](#cite_note-2) As a writer, Alemayehu has used his poetry and storytelling to unite communities over environmental concerns.[[3]](#cite_note-3) Alemayehu is a founding member of the award winning poetry/jazz ensemble Ancestor Energy and a co-founder with Juanita Espinoza of the Native Arts Circle.[[4]](#cite_note-4) Alemayehu participated in [Occupy Minnesota](/wiki/Occupy_Minnesota "Occupy Minnesota") events in 2011.[[5]](#cite_note-5) He serves on the Minneapolis Energy Partnership Board.[[6]](#cite_note-IFYC-6) He is a Buddhist[[6]](#cite_note-IFYC-6) and is the father of 2 daughters, Hanika Mahalia Alemayehu and Eden Luzia Alemayehu.[[7]](#cite_note-7)
