The **Symphony No. 4** of [Roger Sessions](/wiki/Roger_Sessions "Roger Sessions") was composed in 1958.[[1]](#cite_note-1)

It has three [movements](/wiki/Movement_(music) "Movement (music)"):

1. Burlesque. Allegro giocoso[[2]](#cite_note-2)
2. Elegy. Adagio[[3]](#cite_note-3)
3. Pastorale. Andante tranquillo e grazioso, un poco indolente

It was commissioned by the [Minneapolis Symphony Orchestra](/wiki/Minnesota_Orchestra "Minnesota Orchestra") for the Minnesota Centennial, and premiered by the Minnesota Orchestra conducted by [Antal Doráti](/wiki/Antal_Dor%C3%A1ti "Antal Doráti")[[4]](#cite_note-4) on January 2, 1960.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

The second movement's basically slow tempo is interrupted twice by faster episodes. This movement was intended as an elegy for the composer's brother, John, who died in 1948.[[6]](#cite_note-6) The finale, also slow, increases in intensity towards its close.[[7]](#cite_note-7) [Andrea Olmstead](/wiki/Andrea_Olmstead "Andrea Olmstead") describes all of Sessions's symphonies as "serious" and "funereal".[[8]](#cite_note-8)
