House in Porto, Portugal

The **Casa-Oficina António Carneiro** (António Carneiro's House-Workshop) is located in the city of [Porto](/wiki/Porto "Porto"), [Portugal](/wiki/Portugal "Portugal").

## History

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Casa-oficina_Ant%C3%B3nio_Carneiro&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

This house belonged to the [Portuguese painter](/wiki/List_of_Portuguese_painters "List of Portuguese painters") [António Carneiro](/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Carneiro "António Carneiro") and was built in the 1920s. It served as both his residence and workshop. Later on, the same workshop was by his son, Carlos Carneiro, also a painter. Furthermore, his other son, [Cláudio Carneyro](/w/index.php?title=Cl%C3%A1udio_Carneyro&action=edit&redlink=1 "Cláudio Carneyro (page does not exist)"), a [Portuguese composer](/wiki/List_of_Portuguese_composers "List of Portuguese composers"), also lived here.[[1]](#cite_note-1)

After a period in which the house was closed to the public, the building was largely ignored and vandalized.  
 The House-Workshop reopened in 2009, now rehabilitated, with a temporary exhibition of some of the most relevant works of António Carneiro including "A Vida", "Camões" and the interiors of a few churches. The main purpose of this museum is to show work collection of António Carneiro.
