Extinct genus of birds

***Messelornis***, also known as the **Messel rail**, is an [extinct](/wiki/Extinction "Extinction") genus of [gruiform](/wiki/Gruiformes "Gruiformes") [bird](/wiki/Bird "Bird"), closely related to modern [rails](/wiki/Rail_(bird) "Rail (bird)"). It is the most abundant bird from the [Messel](/wiki/Messel_Formation "Messel Formation") [Lagerstätte](/wiki/Lagerst%C3%A4tte "Lagerstätte"), representing roughly half of all Messel bird [fossils](/wiki/Fossil "Fossil") with more than 500 specimens known.[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1)[[3]](#cite_note-Morlo2004-3)[[4]](#cite_note-Mayr2009-4) The fossil record of *Messelornis* ranges from the [Paleocene](/wiki/Paleocene "Paleocene") to the early [Eocene](/wiki/Eocene "Eocene").[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1)[[2]](#cite_note-Mourer-Chaviré1995-2) Both the European species, *M. cristata,* from [Messel](/wiki/Messel "Messel"), and the North American species, *M. nearctica,* from the [Green River Formation](/wiki/Green_River_Formation "Green River Formation") lived during the [Early Eocene Climatic Optimum](/wiki/Early_Eocene_Climatic_Optimum "Early Eocene Climatic Optimum").[[5]](#cite_note-:2-5)[[6]](#cite_note-:1-6)

*Messelornis* was a generalized terrestrial bird, with a short beak, long legs, and a long tail.[[4]](#cite_note-Mayr2009-4) It was probably not an especially adept flier. It was omnivorous and had a diet including seeds, fruits, and fish.[[3]](#cite_note-Morlo2004-3) The name of the type species, *M. cristata*, refers to the fact that it was initially interpreted as having a comb-like crest, but the existence of this crest has since been reinterpreted as unrelated plant material.[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1)[[4]](#cite_note-Mayr2009-4) One of its feathers had an iridescent coloration, but no chemical analysis has been done yet.[[7]](#cite_note-7)

## Taxonomy

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Messelornis&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Taxonomy")]

*Messelornis* was originally interpreted as closely related to the [sunbittern](/wiki/Sunbittern "Sunbittern"), which was also classified as a member of the Gruiformes at the time. However, the sunbittern has since been classified in a separate order, [Eurypygiformes](/wiki/Eurypygiformes "Eurypygiformes"), while *Messelornis* has remained a member of the Gruiformes and is now interpreted as a relative of [rails](/wiki/Rail_(bird) "Rail (bird)").[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1)[[8]](#cite_note-Mayr2004-8)

## Species

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Messelornis&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Species")]

* *M. cristata*, which is the most well represented species of *Messelornis* due to its abundance within the [Eocene](/wiki/Eocene "Eocene") [Messel Pit](/wiki/Messel_Formation "Messel Formation") from which its generic name refers.[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1) Specimens of *M. cristata* display a distinct range of sizes thought to be a result of [sexual dimorphism](/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism_in_birds "Sexual dimorphism in birds"). All known specimens of *M. cristata* are adults, implying that nesting sites were some distance away from the [maar](/wiki/Maar "Maar") lakeshore of ancient [Messel](/wiki/Messel_Formation "Messel Formation").[[5]](#cite_note-:2-5)[[6]](#cite_note-:1-6)
* *M. nearctica*, which is known from the [Eocene](/wiki/Eocene "Eocene") [Green River Formation](/wiki/Green_River_Formation "Green River Formation"). It is distinguished from *M.cristata* by its longer and more slender toes among other skeletal characteristics such as its unique hypotarsus morphology.[[6]](#cite_note-:1-6) Known specimens of *M. nearctica* exhibit a similar size range with *M. cristata* which has been hypothesized to also be the result of [sexual dimorphism](/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism_in_birds "Sexual dimorphism in birds"), but due to the comparative lack of specimens this remains unclear. Similarly, all known specimens of *M. nearctica* are adults as with *M. cristata* which may imply a similar nesting behavior away from the lakeshores of what would become the [Green River Formation](/wiki/Green_River_Formation "Green River Formation").[[6]](#cite_note-:1-6)
* *M. russelli*, which is known from the [Paleocene](/wiki/Paleocene "Paleocene") of France and includes two [humeri](/wiki/Humerus "Humerus") which are poorly preserved.[[1]](#cite_note-:0-1)
