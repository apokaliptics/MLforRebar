Major League Baseball season

Major League Baseball team season

The **1999 St. Louis Cardinals season** was the team's 118th season in [St. Louis, Missouri](/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri "St. Louis, Missouri") and the 108th season in the [National League](/wiki/National_League_(baseball) "National League (baseball)"). The [Cardinals](/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals "St. Louis Cardinals") went 75–86 during the season and finished fourth in the [National League Central](/wiki/National_League_Central "National League Central") division, 21½ games behind the [Houston Astros](/wiki/1999_Houston_Astros_season "1999 Houston Astros season").

## Offseason

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=1999_St._Louis_Cardinals_season&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Offseason")]

* November 19, 1998: Eric Davis was signed as a free agent with the St. Louis Cardinals.[[1]](#cite_note-1)
* November 19, 1998: Ricky Bottalico was traded by the Philadelphia Phillies with Garrett Stephenson to the St. Louis Cardinals for Jeff Brantley, Ron Gant, and Cliff Politte.[[2]](#cite_note-2)
* December 7, 1998: [Willie McGee](/wiki/Willie_McGee "Willie McGee") was signed as a free agent with the St. Louis Cardinals.[[3]](#cite_note-3)
* December 14, 1998: Édgar Rentería was traded by the Florida Marlins to the St. Louis Cardinals for Armando Almanza, Braden Looper, and Pablo Ozuna.[[4]](#cite_note-4)
* January 15, 1999: Mike Mohler was signed as a free agent with the St. Louis Cardinals.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

## Regular season

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=1999_St._Louis_Cardinals_season&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Regular season")]

* On April 23, 1999, Fernando Tatís hit two grand slams in the third inning, both off Chan Ho Park.[[6]](#cite_note-6)
* In 1999, [Mark McGwire](/wiki/Mark_McGwire "Mark McGwire") drove in a league-leading 147 runs while only having 145 hits, the highest RBI-per-hit tally in baseball history.[[7]](#cite_note-7)
