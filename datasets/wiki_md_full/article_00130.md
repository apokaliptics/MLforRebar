Snowboarding event at the 2022 Winter Olympics



The **women's snowboard cross** competition in [snowboarding](/wiki/Snowboarding_at_the_2022_Winter_Olympics "Snowboarding at the 2022 Winter Olympics") at the [2022 Winter Olympics](/wiki/2022_Winter_Olympics "2022 Winter Olympics") was held on 9 February, at the [Genting Snow Park](/wiki/Genting_Snow_Park "Genting Snow Park") in [Zhangjiakou](/wiki/Zhangjiakou "Zhangjiakou").[[1]](#cite_note-1) [Lindsey Jacobellis](/wiki/Lindsey_Jacobellis "Lindsey Jacobellis") of the United States became the Olympic champion. Jacobellis dominated the snowboard cross for almost two decades, winning the [X Games](/wiki/X_Games "X Games") ten times and the [world championships](/wiki/FIS_Snowboard_World_Championships "FIS Snowboard World Championships") six times, but her only Olympic medal so far was the [silver in 2006](/wiki/Snowboarding_at_the_2006_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_snowboard_cross "Snowboarding at the 2006 Winter Olympics – Women's snowboard cross"), when she started celebrating her win too early and was overtaken at the finish line.[[2]](#cite_note-2) [Chloé Trespeuch](/wiki/Chlo%C3%A9_Trespeuch "Chloé Trespeuch") of France, the 2014 bronze medalist, won the silver medal, and [Meryeta O'Dine](/wiki/Meryeta_O%27Dine "Meryeta O'Dine") of Canada the bronze, her first Olympic medal.[[3]](#cite_note-3)[[4]](#cite_note-4)

In the victory ceremony, the medals were presented by [Pál Schmitt](/wiki/P%C3%A1l_Schmitt "Pál Schmitt"), [IOC Member](/wiki/List_of_members_of_the_International_Olympic_Committee "List of members of the International Olympic Committee"), [Hungary](/wiki/Hungary "Hungary"), [Olympian](/wiki/Olympic_Games "Olympic Games"), 2 Golds for [Fencing 1968](/wiki/Fencing_at_the_1968_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e "Fencing at the 1968 Summer Olympics – Men's team épée") and [Fencing 1972](/wiki/Fencing_at_the_1972_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e "Fencing at the 1972 Summer Olympics – Men's team épée"), accompanied by [Johan Eliasch](/wiki/Johan_Eliasch "Johan Eliasch"), [FIS President](/wiki/List_of_presidents_of_FIS "List of presidents of FIS"), [Great Britain](/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom").

## Summary

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Snowboarding_at_the_2022_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_snowboard_cross&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Summary")]

The defending champion was [Michela Moioli](/wiki/Michela_Moioli "Michela Moioli"). The 2018 silver medalist, [Julia Pereira de Sousa Mabileau](/wiki/Julia_Pereira_de_Sousa_Mabileau "Julia Pereira de Sousa Mabileau"), qualified for the Olympics as well. The bronze medalist and the 2014 champion, [Eva Samková](/wiki/Eva_Samkov%C3%A1 "Eva Samková"), was injured and could not participate. At the [2021–22 FIS Snowboard World Cup](/wiki/2021%E2%80%9322_FIS_Snowboard_World_Cup "2021–22 FIS Snowboard World Cup"), six snowboard cross events were held before the Olympics. [Charlotte Bankes](/wiki/Charlotte_Bankes "Charlotte Bankes") was leading the ranking, followed by Trespeuch and Moioli. Bankes was the [2021 world champion](/wiki/FIS_Freestyle_Ski_and_Snowboarding_World_Championships_2021 "FIS Freestyle Ski and Snowboarding World Championships 2021"), with Moioli and Samková being the silver and bronze medalists, respectively.

## Qualification

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Snowboarding_at_the_2022_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_snowboard_cross&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Qualification")]

A total of 32 snowboarders qualified to compete at the games. For an athlete to compete they must have a minimum of 100.00 FIS points on the FIS Points List on 17 January 2022 and a top 30 finish in a World Cup event or at the [FIS Snowboard World Championships 2021](/wiki/FIS_Freestyle_Ski_and_Snowboarding_World_Championships_2021 "FIS Freestyle Ski and Snowboarding World Championships 2021"). A country could enter a maximum of four athletes into the event.[[5]](#cite_note-5)
