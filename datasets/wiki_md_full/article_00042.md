English actor (born 1942)

**John Christopher Dixon** (born 2 December 1942), billed as **John D. Collins**, is a British actor who played Flt. Lt. Fairfax, a stranded British airman in occupied France during [World War II](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), in the [BBC](/wiki/BBC "BBC") sitcom *['Allo 'Allo!](/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo! "'Allo 'Allo!")*. He is the actor to have been cast most frequently in writer/producer [David Croft's](/wiki/David_Croft_(TV_producer) "David Croft (TV producer)") hit sitcoms: a total of six different series and ten characters.

## Biography

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=John_D._Collins&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Biography")]

Collins won the [Ivor Novello](/wiki/Ivor_Novello "Ivor Novello") and [Robert Donat](/wiki/Robert_Donat "Robert Donat") Scholarships to [RADA](/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Dramatic_Art "Royal Academy of Dramatic Art"). After graduating he went on to run the Summer Theatre at [Frinton-on-Sea](/wiki/Frinton-on-Sea "Frinton-on-Sea"), Essex for three years and then went to work in the first season of the [Nottingham Playhouse](/wiki/Nottingham_Playhouse "Nottingham Playhouse").

In his early years he made a number of films including the [Hammer Film](/wiki/Hammer_Film_Productions "Hammer Film Productions") *[Dracula Has Risen from the Grave](/wiki/Dracula_Has_Risen_from_the_Grave "Dracula Has Risen from the Grave")* (1968), the film versions of *[Till Death Us Do Part](/wiki/Till_Death_Us_Do_Part_(film) "Till Death Us Do Part (film)")* (1969) and *[Dad's Army](/wiki/Dad%27s_Army_(1971_film) "Dad's Army (1971 film)")* (1971), *[The Adventures of Barry McKenzie](/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Barry_McKenzie "The Adventures of Barry McKenzie")* (1972), *[The Ghoul](/wiki/The_Ghoul_(1975_film) "The Ghoul (1975 film)")* (1975), and *[The Boys in Blue](/wiki/The_Boys_in_Blue "The Boys in Blue")* (1982), and also many plays for [Granada Television](/wiki/Granada_Television "Granada Television"). As a vicar in *[Coronation Street](/wiki/Coronation_Street "Coronation Street")*, he failed to marry [Albert Tatlock](/wiki/Albert_Tatlock "Albert Tatlock").

For ten years he worked with [Spike Milligan](/wiki/Spike_Milligan "Spike Milligan") as his assistant director and as an actor, in *Son of Oblomov* and *[The Bed-Sitting Room](/wiki/The_Bed-Sitting_Room_(film) "The Bed-Sitting Room (film)")*. He also appeared with Milligan in his ['*Q’ series*](/wiki/Q_(TV_series) "Q (TV series)") on television.

He became a regular cast member of two television series – *[A Family at War](/wiki/A_Family_at_War "A Family at War")* and also with [Robert Lindsay](/wiki/Robert_Lindsay_(actor) "Robert Lindsay (actor)") in *[Get Some In!](/wiki/Get_Some_In! "Get Some In!")*. He first worked for [David Croft](/wiki/David_Croft_(TV_producer) "David Croft (TV producer)") in the film version of *Dad's Army* in 1971, and subsequently was cast in episodes of *[It Ain't Half Hot Mum](/wiki/It_Ain%27t_Half_Hot_Mum "It Ain't Half Hot Mum")*, *[Hi-de-Hi!](/wiki/Hi-de-Hi! "Hi-de-Hi!")*, *[Are You Being Served?](/wiki/Are_You_Being_Served%3F "Are You Being Served?")* and *[Oh, Doctor Beeching!](/wiki/Oh,_Doctor_Beeching! "Oh, Doctor Beeching!")*, as well his regular role in *['Allo 'Allo!](/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo! "'Allo 'Allo!")* and the recurring character of Jerry in *[You Rang, M'Lord?](/wiki/You_Rang,_M%27Lord%3F "You Rang, M'Lord?")*. Along with several other cast members of *'Allo 'Allo*, Collins had previously appeared in *[Secret Army](/wiki/Secret_Army_(TV_series) "Secret Army (TV series)")*, the series of which '*Allo 'Allo* was a parody.

Other TV work includes *[The Brittas Empire](/wiki/The_Brittas_Empire "The Brittas Empire")*, *[On the Up](/wiki/On_the_Up "On the Up")*, *[Birds of a Feather](/wiki/Birds_of_a_Feather_(TV_series) "Birds of a Feather (TV series)")*, *[Trial & Retribution](/wiki/Trial_%26_Retribution "Trial & Retribution")*, *[Peak Practice](/wiki/Peak_Practice "Peak Practice")*, *[Ain't Misbehavin'](/wiki/Ain%27t_Misbehavin%27_(TV_series) "Ain't Misbehavin' (TV series)")*, *[Harry's Mad](/wiki/Harry%27s_Mad_(TV_series) "Harry's Mad (TV series)")*, *[Wycliffe](/wiki/Wycliffe_(TV_series) "Wycliffe (TV series)")*, *[Mosley](/wiki/Mosley_(TV_serial) "Mosley (TV serial)")*, *[Some Mothers Do 'Ave 'Em](/wiki/Some_Mothers_Do_%27Ave_%27Em "Some Mothers Do 'Ave 'Em")*, *[The Sweeney](/wiki/The_Sweeney "The Sweeney")*, *[Secret Army](/wiki/Secret_Army_(TV_series) "Secret Army (TV series)")*, *[Citizen Smith](/wiki/Citizen_Smith "Citizen Smith")*, *[Yes Minister](/wiki/Yes_Minister "Yes Minister")*, the *[Doctor Who](/wiki/Doctor_Who "Doctor Who")* serial *[Arc of Infinity](/wiki/Arc_of_Infinity "Arc of Infinity")*, *[Only Fools and Horses](/wiki/Only_Fools_and_Horses "Only Fools and Horses")* and *[Lovejoy](/wiki/Lovejoy "Lovejoy")*.

Other theatre credits includes the tour of *When Did You Last See Your Trousers?*, *[The Winslow Boy](/wiki/The_Winslow_Boy "The Winslow Boy")*, *That's Showbiz*, *[Richard III](/wiki/Richard_III_(play) "Richard III (play)")* and the title role in *[King Lear](/wiki/King_Lear "King Lear")*. He has also played the role of Mr. Paravicini in the record-breaking production of *[The Mousetrap](/wiki/The_Mousetrap "The Mousetrap")* at [St Martin's Theatre](/wiki/St_Martin%27s_Theatre "St Martin's Theatre"), London.

Collins has appeared in a number of pantomimes as Abanazer and as an ugly sister in *[Cinderella](/wiki/Cinderella "Cinderella")*. He has narrated *[The Snowman](/wiki/The_Snowman "The Snowman")*, *[Carnival of the Animals](/wiki/Carnival_of_the_Animals "Carnival of the Animals")*, *[Peter and the Wolf](/wiki/Peter_and_the_Wolf "Peter and the Wolf")* and [William Walton](/wiki/William_Walton "William Walton")'s *[Façade](/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade_(entertainment) "Façade (entertainment)")* and has also recorded a Shakespearean CD with the Gesualdo Consort. He has just played the voice of God in *[Noye's Fludde](/wiki/Noye%27s_Fludde "Noye's Fludde")* by [Benjamin Britten](/wiki/Benjamin_Britten "Benjamin Britten") and has been appearing regularly as [Henry VIII](/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England "Henry VIII of England") at the banquets in the Old Palace at [Hatfield House](/wiki/Hatfield_House "Hatfield House").

Collins made two appearances in the British BBC sitcom *[Only Fools and Horses](/wiki/Only_Fools_and_Horses "Only Fools and Horses")*, initially in the episode "[Ashes to Ashes](/wiki/Ashes_to_Ashes_(Only_Fools_and_Horses) "Ashes to Ashes (Only Fools and Horses)")", as a river policeman[[1]](#cite_note-1) who informs Del and Rodney that they have to have river permission to empty ashes into a river (St. Katharine Docks). He then appeared in the later episode "[Sleeping Dogs Lie](/wiki/Sleeping_Dogs_Lie_(Only_Fools_and_Horses) "Sleeping Dogs Lie (Only Fools and Horses)")" as the Veterinarian.

Collins has also appeared in BBC children's programme, including [ChuckleVision](/wiki/ChuckleVision "ChuckleVision") as a flying instructor in the 1996 episode Dear Diary.
