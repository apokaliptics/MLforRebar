2021 American nature documentary

***The Loneliest Whale: The Search for 52*** is a 2021 American [documentary film](/wiki/Documentary_film "Documentary film"), directed, written, and produced by [Joshua Zeman](/wiki/Joshua_Zeman "Joshua Zeman"). [Leonardo DiCaprio](/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio "Leonardo DiCaprio") and [Adrian Grenier](/wiki/Adrian_Grenier "Adrian Grenier") serve as executive producers. The film follows a group of scientists in search of the [52-hertz whale](/wiki/52-hertz_whale "52-hertz whale"), a whale which is believed to have spent its life in solitude, calling out at a frequency different from any other whale. It was commercially released in theaters and on video on demand by [Bleecker Street](/wiki/Bleecker_Street_(company) "Bleecker Street (company)") on July 9, 2021.[[2]](#cite_note-deadline-2)

## Synopsis

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Synopsis")]

The film follows Zeman and a team of [oceanographers](/wiki/Oceanography "Oceanography") and scientists on a quest to find the "[52-hertz whale](/wiki/52-hertz_whale "52-hertz whale")", which scientists believe has spent its entire life in solitude calling out at a frequency that is different from any other whale. Zeman also explores the Cold War science of submarine warfare that led to the original discovery of the 52-hertz whale, the negative impact of whaling, the release and reception of the *[Songs of the Humpback Whale](/wiki/Songs_of_the_Humpback_Whale_(album) "Songs of the Humpback Whale (album)")* record, the detrimental effects of ocean noise on whale and their communication, and how society's dependence on technology can lead to feelings of isolation among people.[[3]](#cite_note-nytimes-3)

## Cast

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Cast")]

It features the following scientists and oceanographers:

* Ana Sirovic — Associate Professor at Texas A&M University at Galveston
* David Rothenberg — Musician and philosopher, Distinguished Professor at the [New Jersey Institute of Technology](/wiki/New_Jersey_Institute_of_Technology "New Jersey Institute of Technology")
* Joe George — Retired Navy Ocean Systems Technician Analyst
* John Calambokidis — Senior Research Biologist and one of the founders of Cascadia Research Collective[[4]](#cite_note-4)
* John Hildebrand — Distinguished Professor at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography of the University of California at San Diego

## Production

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Production")]

In February 2015, it was announced Joshua Zeman would direct a documentary film revolving around the [52-hertz whale](/wiki/52-hertz_whale "52-hertz whale"), with [Adrian Grenier](/wiki/Adrian_Grenier "Adrian Grenier") set to executive produce, and crowdfunded through [Kickstarter](/wiki/Kickstarter "Kickstarter").[[5]](#cite_note-5) In March 2015, it was announced [Leonardo DiCaprio](/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio "Leonardo DiCaprio") had joined the film, as an executive producer under his [Appian Way Productions](/wiki/Appian_Way_Productions "Appian Way Productions") banner.[[6]](#cite_note-6)[[7]](#cite_note-7)

## Release

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Release")]

In March 2021, [Bleecker Street](/wiki/Bleecker_Street_(company) "Bleecker Street (company)") acquired U.S. distribution rights to the film.[[2]](#cite_note-deadline-2) The film had its world premiere at the [Nantucket Film Festival](/wiki/Nantucket_Film_Festival "Nantucket Film Festival") on June 17, 2021.[[8]](#cite_note-8) It was released in a [limited release](/wiki/Limited_release "Limited release") on July 9, 2021, prior to [video on demand](/wiki/Video_on_demand "Video on demand") on July 16, 2021.[[9]](#cite_note-9) On October 12, 2021, the film was released on [Hulu](/wiki/Hulu "Hulu").[[10]](#cite_note-10)

## Reception

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Reception")]

### Critical response

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=The_Loneliest_Whale:_The_Search_for_52&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Critical response")]

On [Rotten Tomatoes](/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes "Rotten Tomatoes"), the series holds an approval rating of 88% based on 42 reviews, with an average rating of 7.2/10. On [Metacritic](/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic"), the series holds a rating of 69 out of 100, based on 7 critics, indicating "generally favorable" reviews.[[11]](#cite_note-11)[[12]](#cite_note-12)

In the *[LA Times](/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times "Los Angeles Times")*, Katie Walsh called the film "a modern-day Moby Dick with a conservationist bent" that "surprises, delights and will keep you on the edge of your seat."[[13]](#cite_note-13) Sheri Linden of *[The Hollywood Reporter](/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter "The Hollywood Reporter")* called it "a well-rounded overview" with "bright buoyancy", and Jeannette Catsoulis of *[The New York Times](/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")* called it a "piquant" and "unexpectedly endearing... bobbing meditation on our own species' environmental arrogance."[[14]](#cite_note-14)[[3]](#cite_note-nytimes-3) Stephanie Zacharek of *[Time](/wiki/Time_(magazine) "Time (magazine)")* said it was "both invigorating and calming to watch" while still being "surprisingly exciting." Carlos Aguilar of *[The Wrap](/wiki/The_Wrap "The Wrap")* said it was an "engaging exploration" with "a satisfying conclusion that's never too convenient."[[15]](#cite_note-15)[[16]](#cite_note-16)
