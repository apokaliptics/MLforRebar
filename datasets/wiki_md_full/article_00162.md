Species of moth

***Ypsolopha falciferella*** is a species of [moth](/wiki/Moth "Moth") in the family [Ypsolophidae](/wiki/Ypsolophidae "Ypsolophidae") [first described](/wiki/Species_description "Species description") by [Lord Walsingham](/wiki/Thomas_de_Grey,_6th_Baron_Walsingham "Thomas de Grey, 6th Baron Walsingham") in 1881.[[1]](#cite_note-1) It is found throughout much of North America, including [British Columbia](/wiki/British_Columbia "British Columbia"), [Alberta](/wiki/Alberta "Alberta"), [Manitoba](/wiki/Manitoba "Manitoba"), [Saskatchewan](/wiki/Saskatchewan "Saskatchewan"), [Michigan](/wiki/Michigan "Michigan") and [Maryland](/wiki/Maryland "Maryland"). It is known from a wide variety of habitats, including mixed wood forests, riparian habitats and semi-arid scrubland.[[2]](#cite_note-2)

The [wingspan](/wiki/Wingspan "Wingspan") is about 21 mm. Adults are on wing from June to beginning of October.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

The larvae feed on *[Prunus virginiana](/wiki/Prunus_virginiana "Prunus virginiana")*. Pupation takes place in an elongated silken cocoon.
