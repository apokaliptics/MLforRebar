Italian comic book artist

**Bruno Brindisi** (born 3 June 1964) is an Italian comic book artist.

## Biography

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Bruno_Brindisi&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Biography")]

Brindisi was born at [Salerno](/wiki/Salerno "Salerno"), and debuted in the amateur publication *Truemoon*. Later, he started drawing [erotic comics](/wiki/Erotic_comics "Erotic comics") for Blue Press and [Ediperiodici](/w/index.php?title=Ediperiodici&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ediperiodici (page does not exist)").

In 1991, he entered the regular staff of the horror series *[Dylan Dog](/wiki/Dylan_Dog "Dylan Dog")*, published by [Sergio Bonelli Editore](/wiki/Sergio_Bonelli_Editore "Sergio Bonelli Editore"), debuting with #51, entitled "Il male". For the same publisher, Brindisi also realised episodes of [Nick Raider](/wiki/Nick_Raider "Nick Raider") and a special for [Tex Willer](/wiki/Tex_Willer "Tex Willer") (2002), written by [Claudio Nizzi](/wiki/Claudio_Nizzi "Claudio Nizzi"). For *Dylan Dog*, Brindisi was selected in particular as the artist for the 200th issue and for the 20th anniversary celebrative one. As of November 2014[[update]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bruno_Brindisi&action=edit), Brindisi has drawn a total of 39 Dylan Dog stories, including regular and special series.

In 2005-2006, he drew two albums for the French series *Novikov*, with script by [Patrick Weber](/w/index.php?title=Patrick_Weber&action=edit&redlink=1 "Patrick Weber (page does not exist)").

In 2012, he drew for Astorina the comic book *Il segreto di [Diabolik](/wiki/Diabolik "Diabolik")*. In December of the same year, again for Bonelli, he drew the one-shot *La rivolta dei [Sepoy](/wiki/Sepoy "Sepoy")*, written by Giuseppe De Nardo for the series *Le storie*.

In 2013, he became the regular cover artist for *Dylan Dog - Collezione storica a colori*, a series reprinting Dylan Dog stories in colour.
