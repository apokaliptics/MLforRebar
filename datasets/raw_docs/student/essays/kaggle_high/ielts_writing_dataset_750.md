The pie graphs depict the nutritional consistency of medium baked potato and macaroni as dinner.
Overall, carbohydrates and protein contributed to a substantial amount of nutrition in both macaroni and medium baked potato, whereas glucose and other nutrients made up a small proportion. In addition to that, the saturated fat in macaroni is drastically higher than in medium baked potato.
It is evident that the percentage of carbohydrates was up to 52% in macaroni compared to 35% in the medium baked potato. The proportion of the protein in medium baked potato accounted for 25% which is more than two times higher than the protein in macaroni (11%). However, this trend reversed for the percentage of saturated fat as there are only 10% in the medium baked potato and 21% in the macaroni.
In terms of glucose, both medium baked potato and macaroni consisted of a similar amount, 15% and 11% respectively. Moreover, there was 15% of other nutrients contained in medium baked potato while the figure in macaroni was only 5%.
