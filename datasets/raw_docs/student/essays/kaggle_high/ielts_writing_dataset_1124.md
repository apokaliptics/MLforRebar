The bar chart sheds light on the number of traffic accidents and resulting injuries by six transport types per 100 million passenger miles travelled in 2002.
In general, apart from a majority of traffic incidents and injuries caused by unknown transport methods, most incidents took place bus travels and light rail travels. Also, the accidents caused by bus travels resulted in most injuries.
As shown in this graph, over 250 accidents were engendered by unknown vehicles, leading to nearly 180 injuries. Aside from that, the traffic vehicles, light rail and bus, were related to most accidents, with approximately 80 incidents caused by each type of vehicle respectively. In addition, the accidents involved in bus travels led to over 50 injuries while those in other types of travels, namely, light rail, heavy rail and communter rail, gave rise to less than 50 injuries. On the contrary, communter rail was the safest method of travel, involved in about 20 accidents and 20 injuries.
